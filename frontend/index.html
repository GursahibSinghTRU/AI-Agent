Policy & Governance Resources

TRU's governance framework supports transparency, accountability, and ethical decision-making across all university operations. Browse the policy categories below or ask the AI Policy Assistant for an instant, sourced answer.

Records Management & Destruction
Governing the creation, retention, and secure destruction of university records. Covers physical and electronic records, retention schedules, and approved destruction methods including shredding, burning, and secure digital deletion.
Human Resources & Employment
Employment policies covering conflict of interest, conduct expectations, performance management, leave entitlements, and harassment-free workplace requirements for faculty and staff.
Finance & Procurement
Policies on travel expense reimbursement, purchasing goods and services, spending authority levels, supplier contracts, and financial controls applicable to all university departments.
Academic Integrity & Student Conduct
Standards of academic honesty, plagiarism definitions, consequences of misconduct, and the formal process for addressing academic integrity violations for students and instructors.
Health, Safety & Environment
Occupational health and safety obligations, incident reporting procedures, safe work practices, environmental compliance, and the university's Joint Occupational Health & Safety Committee mandate.
Privacy, IT & Acceptable Use
Responsible use of university IT resources, data privacy obligations under FIPPA, password and system access requirements, and acceptable use guidelines for university networks and software.
