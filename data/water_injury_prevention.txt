# Water Safety & Drowning Prevention Data (British Columbia)

## Overview
- **Incidence:** Drowning is a leading cause of death for children; near-drowning can result in lifelong health effects (e.g., brain damage).
- **B.C. Fatalities:** Average of 76 drowning deaths annually; males constitute 78% of these fatalities.
- **Seasonality:** The majority of incidents occur during the summer months.
- **Economic Impact:** Total 2023 costs were estimated at $19 million ($17 million indirect/lost productivity, $1.9 million direct/healthcare).

## Demographic Vulnerabilities
- **Locations:** Almost 1/4 of all Canadian drownings occur in bathtubs and pools.
- **At-Risk Groups:**
  - Young children (can drown in 2.5 cm/1 inch of water in seconds).
  - Adults aged 65+ years.
  - Almost half of child drownings occur in backyard swimming pools.
- **Supervision:** 92% of child drownings occur when no parent or supervisor is present, or when present but distracted.

## Risk Factors
- Failure to wear a lifejacket or Personal Floatation Device (PFD) when appropriate.
- Being alone or swimming alone.
- Alcohol consumption during water activities or while supervising.
- Being a weak or non-swimmer.
- Ignoring cold water shock risks.

## Prevention Strategies

### Home & Residential
- **Supervision:** An adult must be within arms-reach of young children at all times near or in water.
- **Pool Fencing:** Install a 4-sided fence (minimum 4 ft tall) with a self-closing, self-latching gate. Fencing can prevent 7 out of 10 drownings among children.
- **Access Control:** Pools should not be accessible directly from the house.
- **Emergency Preparedness:** Maintain a first aid kit and phone in the immediate pool area.

### Recreational & Boating
- **Equipment:** Ensure everyone wears an approved lifejacket or PFD on boats, at beaches, or near lakes.
  - *Lifejacket vs. PFD:* Lifejackets provide more floatation and turn the body to keep the face out of water; PFDs are lighter for recreational use.
- **Licensing:** A Boating License/Pleasure Craft Operating Card is required for operating powered watercraft in B.C.
- **Alcohol Policy:** Do not consume alcohol before or during swimming or boating, or when responsible for supervising children.
- **Education:** Enroll in swimming lessons, CPR, and lifesaving techniques training.
- **Hiking/Swim Caution:** Stay on trails; avoid fast-flowing rivers and dangerous currents.
