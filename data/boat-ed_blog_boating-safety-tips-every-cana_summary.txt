# Canadian Boating Safety & Compliance Guidelines

## Legal Requirements
- **Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC):** Mandatory proof of competency required for operating most motorized vessels in Canada. Must be carried while boating.
- **Navigation Rules:** Boaters must understand right-of-way, buoy markers, speed zones, and the Rules of the Road to avoid collisions.
- **Vessel Capacity:** Adhere strictly to the vessel's capacity plate. Overloading increases capsizing risk and destabilizes the boat.

## Personal Safety & Equipment
- **Personal Flotation Devices (PFDs):** All occupants must wear or have immediate access to a Transport Canada-approved PFD that fits them.
- **Safety Gear Inventory:** Boats must carry sound-signaling devices, navigation lights, bailers/bilge pumps, flashlights, and fire extinguishers. Requirements vary by vessel size.
- **Paddlecraft:** Non-motorized vessels (canoes, kayaks) are vulnerable to capsizing and require PFDs, whistles, and bailers.

## Operational Hazards & Risk Mitigation
- **Cold Water Awareness:** Hypothermia can occur in minutes even during summer. Dress for water temperature, not air temperature.
- **Weather Monitoring:** Check marine forecasts before departure. Monitor VHF radio or weather apps. Remain on shore if conditions are doubtful.
- **Substance Control:** Operating under the influence of alcohol is illegal and dangerous. Designate a sober skipper.
- **Communication:** Maintain fully charged communication devices (phone, marine radio on VHF Channel 16). Use satellite messengers or PLBs for remote areas.

## Emergency Preparedness
- **Float Plan:** File a plan detailing destination, passengers, and expected return time. Share with someone on shore.
- **Self-Rescue:** In remote areas, carry proper communication gear and learn self-rescue techniques.
- **Equipment Maintenance:** Regularly inspect and maintain all safety equipment to ensure functionality during emergencies.
