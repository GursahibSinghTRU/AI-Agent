# Trail Etiquette & Safety Guidelines
**Source:** Mountain Biking BC  
**Topic:** Risk Management, User Conduct, and Trail Stewardship

## General Conduct & Safety
*   **Safety Gear:** Always wear a helmet and appropriate safety gear.
*   **Equipment & Ability:** Know your equipment, personal ability, and the riding area. Plan ahead and be self-sufficient (supplies for weather changes).
*   **Bicycle Control:** Maintain full control of the bicycle. Obey speed regulations and ride within limits to prevent social conflicts or accidents.
*   **Communication:** Announce presence with a greeting or bell ring; anticipate other users around corners.

## Trail Usage & Environmental Safety
*   **Authorized Access:** Ride open trails only. Respect closures, obtain necessary permits, and do not trespass on private land.
*   **Protected Areas:** Do not trespass in state or federal Wilderness areas. Adhere to bicycle access restrictions in Provincial and National Parks.
*   **Leave No Trace:** Stay on existing trails; do not cut switchbacks or widen trails (especially around standing water).
*   **Trail Damage:** Wet and muddy trails are vulnerable. Consider alternate routes when trails are soft. Pack out trash and litter.

## Right of Way Hierarchy
*   **Bicyclist vs. Pedestrian:** Bicycles generally yield to hikers on shared trails. On bike-only trails, hikers must yield to bikers.
*   **Bicyclist vs. Bicyclist:** Uphill traffic has the right of way on two-way trails unless signed otherwise (e.g., downhill-only).
*   **Bicyclist vs. Horse:** Bicycles must yield to horses. **Safety Protocol:** Stop, dismount, and walk on the downhill side of the animal when passing. Provide distance and time for adjustment.
*   **Bicyclist vs. Vehicle:** Motorized vehicles should yield to bicyclists on shared trails.

## Risk Mitigation Notes
*   **Speed Management:** Excessive speed is a primary cause of social conflicts and accidents.
*   **Wildlife:** Never scare animals. Running cattle and disturbing wildlife are considered serious offenses.
*   **Ambassadorship:** Riders should act as good ambassadors to ensure trail longevity and sport safety.
