# Water Safety Knowledge Base: Open Water Swimming (BC Context)

## Key Risk Factors
- **Open Water vs. Pool:** Swimming skills in pools do not directly translate to open water. Open water involves variables including tides, currents, and cold temperatures.
- **Cold Water:** Ocean water temperatures are often cold and can affect movement and equilibrium.
- **Underwater Hazards:** Underwater currents may differ from surface appearance; debris, objects, and vessels can appear quickly.
- **Visibility Risks:** Swimmers are "invisible" to boats, kayakers, and other water users.
- **Sobriety:** Alcohol and drugs significantly impair water safety.

## Safety Protocols
- **Swim Buddy Rule:** Never swim alone.
- **Lifeguard Presence:** Always swim in open water locations where lifeguards are present.
- **Visibility:** Wear bright-colored bathing suits and personal flotation devices (PFDs) to ensure visibility.
- **Skill Development:** Learn basic swimming skills in a pool before attempting open water.

## Drowning Statistics (BC)
- **2023 Data:** 101 accidental drowning deaths reported in B.C.
- **Residency:** 85% of accidental drowning victims in recent years were known to reside in B.C.
- **Newcomer Trend:** There is an observed increase in non-fatal drowning rescues involving newcomers to the province, indicating a lack of local water safety training.

## Recommended Resources
- **Swim to Survive:** Lifesaving Society of B.C. program.
  - **Format:** National standard teaching minimum survival skills.
  - **Cost:** Free sessions.
  - **Language:** Offered in multiple languages with translators.
  - **Locations:** Metro Vancouver (Delta, Surrey, Richmond).
  - **Audience:** All ages, family-friendly, includes women-only sessions.
- **Vancouver Openwater Swim Association (VOWSA):**
  - Offers 90-minute supervised practice swims.
  - Lifeguard supervision via boats in specific areas.
  - Requires sign-in/out procedures.
- **Training Location Note:** Learning to swim is recommended in a pool environment rather than open water.
