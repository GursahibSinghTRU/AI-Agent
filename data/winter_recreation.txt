# Winter Sports and Recreation Safety Guidelines

## General Safety Protocols
- **Protective Equipment:** Wear sport-certified helmets. Supplement with wrist guards, goggles, gloves, and padding.
- **Attire:** Dress in layers to prevent frostbite/hypothermia. Use neck warmers instead of scarves. Remove drawstrings from clothing.
- **Skin & Hydration:** Apply sunscreen for UV protection (required even on cloudy days). Hydrate before and during activity.
- **Supervision:** Actively monitor young children during outdoor activities.
- **Clothing Maintenance:** Change wet clothing immediately to prevent cold injury.

## Ice Safety (Skating)
- **Thickness Requirements:** Minimum 15 cm (6 in) for solo skating; 20 cm (8 in) for groups.
- **Inspection:** Verify ice thickness each session; conditions change rapidly.
- **Restricted Areas:** Avoid open water pockets and storm water management ponds. Consult local experts on area hazards.

## Sledding/Tobogganing Safety
- **Body Position:** Kneel or sit upright.
- **Prohibited Positions:** Do not sled headfirst (risk of head injury) or lie flat on back (risk of spinal injury).
- **Location:** Use designated sledding areas. Avoid roads, parking lots, rocks, trees, goal posts, and fences.

## Skiing and Snowboarding Safety
- **Equipment Check:** Inspect gear condition and fit before each use.
- **Professional Service:** Have bindings checked annually by a professional.
- **Snowboard Specifics:** Ensure full-length steel edges, sturdy bindings, and a securely attached board leash.
- **Eye Protection:** Use sunglasses or goggles with UV protection against snow glare.
