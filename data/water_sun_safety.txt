# BC Water and Heat Safety Protocols

## Drowning Statistics & Risk Factors
- **BC Incidence:** Average of 78 drowning deaths per year.
- **Peak Season:** Summer months.
- **High-Risk Demographics:** Young males, children under 5 years.
- **Event Nature:** Drownings can occur quickly and quietly; victims may not appear to struggle visibly.
- **Drowning Risk:** Very small children can drown in as little as 2.5 cm (1 inch) of water.

## Drowning Prevention Strategies
- **Swim Conditions:** Avoid large waves and strong undertows. Only swim in designated areas with lifeguards present.
- **Supervision:** 
  - Actively supervise all children in or around water.
  - Young children/weak swimmers must be within arms-length for "touch supervision."
  - Keep supervisors free from distractions (e.g., put phones away).
- **Equipment:** 
  - All persons on a boat must wear a PFD (Personal Flotation Device).
  - **PFD vs. Lifejacket:** PFDs are lighter/recreational; Lifejackets turn unconscious wearers over. Automatic inflatable PFDs approved only for persons over 16.
- **Substance Use:** Prohibit alcohol or drug use before or during swimming/boating.

## Heat Illness & Sun Safety
- **Heat Exhaustion Signs:** Headache, dizziness, confusion, unsteadiness, loss of thirst, rapid breathing/heartbeat, nausea, vomiting, decreased urination (dark urine).
- **Protection:** 
  - Apply sunscreen (SPF 50+) and reapply frequently, especially after water contact.
  - Wear sun hats, sunglasses, and lightweight, breathable clothing.
  - Children should wear long sleeves for extra sun protection.
- **Hydration:** Drink water before feeling thirsty. Consume cooling foods (e.g., watermelon, cucumber).
- **Timing:** Schedule outdoor activities for cooler parts of the day; avoid peak heat (~5 PM).
- **Vulnerable Monitoring:** Check on older adults, pregnant individuals, those with limited mobility, or individuals living alone.

## Child Play Safety & Resilience
- **Resilience Building:** Support children in learning to cope with failure and overcome fear without immediate intervention.
- **Safety Boundaries:** Enforce play restrictions near busy streets, unsupervised large water bodies, firearms, trampolines, and motorized vehicles (e.g., ATVs).
- **Safety Equipment:** Use helmets for biking and other activities.
- **Parental Approach:** 
  - Let children lead on play preferences.
  - Encourage participation in challenging activities using safety equipment.
  - Sit on hands to prevent instinctive rescue during minor falls, allowing child to attempt recovery.
