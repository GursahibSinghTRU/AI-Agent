# Pet Safety Guidelines: Weather & Hazard Mitigation

## Heat Exposure Risks
- **Vehicle Safety:** Never leave pets in parked vehicles. Interior temperatures can reach 120°F within minutes.
- **Pickup Trucks:** Driving with dogs in the bed is dangerous. Use a secure crate in the bed or a seat belt harness in the cab.
- **Sun Exposure:** Pets can sunburn. Apply vet-approved sunscreen to light-furred areas (ears, nose).
- **Exercise:** Limit activity on hot days. Avoid walking on hot asphalt. Provide ample shade and water.
- **Parasites:** Use only veterinary-recommended flea and tick treatments. Over-the-counter products may be toxic.

## Cold Exposure Risks
- **General Safety:** No breed is immune to cold. All pets require shelter and insulation against wind/chill.
- **Frostbite:** Monitor ears and paws in freezing weather. Immediate vet attention is required for frostbite.
- **Small Breeds:** Short-coated breeds (e.g., Chihuahuas) are at higher risk in extreme cold.
- **Hydration:** Check outdoor water bowls frequently; use heated bowls to prevent freezing.
- **Snow Safety:** Do not leash off during snowstorms to prevent getting lost or disoriented.
- **De-icing:** Wipe paws after walking on salted surfaces. Trim fur between paw pads to prevent ice trapping.

## Toxic Substances & Hazards
- **Antifreeze:** A teaspoon is lethal. Can cause kidney failure within 24 hours. Flush radiator drainage spots immediately.
- **Plants:** More than 700 plants are toxic. Avoid poisonous holiday plants (poinsettias, holly, mistletoe).
- **Holidays:** Keep cats and dogs away from tinsel, chocolate, and loose decorations.
- **Cats:** Prevent access under vehicles before starting engines to avoid injury from fans or exhaust.

## Identification & Travel
- **ID Tags:** Pets must wear collars with identification tags.
- **Crowds:** Do not take pets to loud or crowded summer events; noise and heat cause stress.
- **Travel:** Research airline pet embargoes and transit policies before booking.

## Emergency Contacts (Winnipeg Region)
- **Humane Society Emergency Line:** 204-982-2020
- **Police Non-Emergency Line:** 204-986-6222
- **Protocol:** If a pet is found in a vehicle and owner is absent, contact emergency line or police immediately.
- **Heat Stroke:** If signs of heatstroke are visible, do not attempt to drive to the vehicle alone if conditions are extreme; alert business management first.
