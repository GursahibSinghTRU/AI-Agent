# BC Hiking Safety Guidelines

## The 10 Essentials
All hikers must carry the following items on outings:
- **Navigation & Light:** Map & compass, headlamp or flashlight.
- **Supplies:** Extra water, extra food, extra clothes.
- **Safety:** First-aid supplies, fire starter supplies, repair kit & tools.
- **Emergency:** Emergency shelter (blanket).
- **Sun Protection:** Sunscreen/hat/sunglasses.

## Trip Planning & Communication
- **Research:** Use guidebooks/websites to match trail difficulty with experience and fitness. Check weather and avalanche risk (www.avalanche.ca).
- **Trip Plan:** Leave detailed itinerary with a contact person; notify them of arrival. Complete trailhead registration if available.
- **Resources:** www.mountainclubs.org, www.adventuresmart.ca.

## Group Safety
- **Group Composition:** Inexperienced hikers must never hike alone. Join clubs (e.g., BCMC) to access experienced groups.
- **Group Discipline:** Do not race ahead or leave slower members behind.

## Gear Recommendations
- **Clothing:** Synthetic/multiple thin layers, waterproof breathable outer shell, lug-soled boots.
- **Stability:** Trekking poles are recommended for knee/back stability, balance, and creek crossings.
- **Communication:** Cell phone (verify coverage), satellite phone, or Spot Tracker (carry per group).
- **Signaling:** Flares, bright colored sheet, mirror, whistle.
- **Snow:** Avalanche beacon, probe, and shovel required.

## Wildlife & Conduct
- **Bear Safety:** Make noise in bear country, keep camp clean, hang food, carry bear spray & bear bangers.
- **Etiquette:** Do not crowd others, slow down when passing, keep noise down, no littering.

## Skill Development
- **Navigation:** Learn to use map, compass, GPS, and create waypoints.
- **Training:** Take courses on avalanche assessment, rescue, mountaineering, or survival (www.mountainclubs.org).
- **Orienteering:** Utilize resources from Federation of Mountain Clubs of B.C. (www.mountainclubs.org).
