# Winter Driving Safety & Risk Management (BC Context)

## Hazard Assessment
- **Classification:** Winter driving is a confirmed work hazard.
- **Risk Requirements:** Risk assessments must include winter driving conditions.
- **Environmental Factors:** Snow, ice, rain, and fog can arrive suddenly; planning must begin year-round.

## Employer Legal Responsibilities
- **Duty of Care:** Employers must assess and reduce risks for employees driving on the job.
- **Preparation:** Use provided templates to plan ahead for winter.
- **Scope:** Applies to employers, supervisors, and commercial carriers.

## Vehicle & Driver Specifications
- **Tire Regulations:** Adherence to BC winter tire regulations required for wet, snowy, or icy roads.
- **Vehicle Maintenance:** Check tires, top up fluids, and verify general vehicle readiness before winter.
- **Emergency Kit:** Drivers must pack a winter emergency kit.

## Training & Resources
- **Workplace Program:** "Making Winter Driving Safer for Your Employees" Online Course.
- **Individual Driver Training:** "Become a Safer Winter Driver" Online Course.
- **Guides & Tools:**
  - Winter Driving Safety Planning Tool Kit.
  - Tailgate Meeting Guides (covers tire specs, weather conditions, etc.).
  - Winter Driving Safety Tip Sheets.
- **Supporting Organization:** Road Safety at Work Alliance (provides resources for roadside worker protection).
