# Winter Driving & Snowmobile Safety Guidelines (Transport Canada)

## Vehicle Preparation
- **Snow Clearance:** Remove all snow from vehicle, roof, hood, windows, and lights before driving.
- **Tires:** Transport Canada recommends winter tires on all wheels for cold, snowy, or icy conditions (better traction due to softer rubber/tread design).
- **Emergency Kit:** Keep phone charged with a cable; carry emergency supplies.

## Driving Protocols
- **Attire:** Wear seat belts at all times, even with bulky coats. Ensure child car seat compatibility with winter clothing per manual.
- **Substances:** Do not drive after consuming alcohol or cannabis.
- **Visibility:** Turn on headlights in poor visibility conditions.
- **Speed:** Match speed to road/weather; avoid passing or lane changes in poor conditions.
- **Technology:** Avoid cruise control in extreme weather; do not rely on driver assistance systems (blind spot, lane keep) in bad weather.

## Braking & Skid Management
- **Following Distance:** Increase gap between vehicles for longer stopping distances.
- **Braking:** Maintain brake pressure if vehicle is out of control; do not release pedal.
- **Skid Prevention:** Slow down, avoid sudden wheel movement or forceful braking.
- **ESC:** Electronic Stability Control is standard (2012+); allows intervention to prevent skidding. Turn off only if stuck.

## Snowmobile Safety
- **Preparation:** Maintain vehicle, learn controls, take safety training, and study route/hazards.
- **Gear & Rules:** Wear proper safety gear (helmet); follow local trail rules and etiquette.
- **Environment:** Check weather, understand avalanche/ice safety, and carry first aid.
- **Sobriety:** Ride sober; ride in a group if possible.

## Emergency Response
- **Contact:** Call 911 in emergencies.
- **Shelter:** If driving becomes unsafe, pull over to rest areas or buildings.
