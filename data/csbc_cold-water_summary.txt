# Cold Water Immersion Safety Guidelines

**The 1–10–1 Rule of Cold Water Immersion**
*   **1 Minute: Cold Shock**
    *   Initial deep gasp followed by severe hyperventilation (600–1000% increase in breathing).
    *   Primary Risk: Airway obstruction leading to drowning.
    *   Action: Control breathing, avoid panic. Critical to wear a lifejacket to stay afloat.
*   **10 Minutes: Cold Incapacitation**
    *   Loss of motor function in fingers, arms, and legs.
    *   Swim failure occurs within this window.
    *   Action: Maintain airway clearance; prepare for rescue if self-rescue is impossible.
*   **1 Hour: Hypothermia**
    *   Risk of unconsciousness and loss of consciousness.
    *   Action: Utilize rescue techniques and heat conservation to delay onset.

**Physiological Factors**
*   **Heat Loss Rate:** Body heat is lost 25 times faster in water than air at similar temperatures.
*   **Movement Impact:** Swimming or moving in water can increase heat loss by up to 10 times.

**Prevention and Equipment**
*   **Essential Gear:** Wear a Coast Guard-approved lifejacket. Use thermal protection (floater jacket/pants or survival suit) for maximum insulation.
*   **Risk Reduction:** Prevent overloading boats, avoid situations prone to falling overboard, and do not operate when water is cold without proper gear.

**Survival Techniques**
*   **Heat Conservation:** Draw legs to chest, wrap arms around them (tuck position).
*   **Group Survival:** Huddle together as close as possible to conserve heat.
*   **Priority:** Focus on maintaining flotation and airway clearance rather than swimming.
