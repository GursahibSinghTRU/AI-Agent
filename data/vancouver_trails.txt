# Hiking Safety Protocols (Vancouver/BC Region)

## Communication & Emergency Prep
- **Itinerary:** Always inform others of the specific trail, location, and expected return time.
- **Cell Service:** Do not rely on cellphones. Reception is unreliable in remote BC forests/mountains.

## Essential Gear (The 10 Essentials + Safety)
- **Navigation:** Compass and maps (waterproofed). GPS is supplementary only.
- **Lighting:** Headlamp/flashlight with spare batteries for pre-sunset travel.
- **Signaling:** Loud whistle (e.g., Fox 40).
- **Fire:** Waterproof matches/lighter in a plastic bag.
- **Shelter:** Thermal tarp or bright-colored emergency plastic bag.
- **Sustenance:** Extra high-energy food bars and ample water. **Do not** drink from running rivers/glaciers (bacterial risk).
- **Clothing:** Extra layers (jacket, gloves) for overnight weather changes.
- **First Aid:** Kit including bandages, gauze, blister dressings, gloves.
- **Tools:** Quality pocket knife for shelter/fire construction.
- **Sun Protection:** Sunscreen, sunglasses, hat.

## Hiking Conduct
- **Group Size:** Never hike alone. Hike with a friend or group.
- **Footwear:** Wear sturdy, comfortable shoes for rocky/muddy terrain.
- **Timing:** Complete hike before darkness.
- **Weather:** Check forecasts; rain creates slip hazards on steep/rocky trails.

## Wildlife Safety (Bears)
- **Habitat:** Black bears common; Grizzly bears present.
- **Protocol:** Make noise to alert bears of presence.
- **Encounter:** Calmly turn the other direction and walk away.
- **Defense:** Carry and use bear spray/mace.
- **Reference:** Consult Get Bear Smart Society guidelines.
