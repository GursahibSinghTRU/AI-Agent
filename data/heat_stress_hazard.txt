# Heat Stress: Risk & Safety Guide (WorkSafeBC)

## Definition & Responsibility
- **Condition:** Heat stress occurs when the body heats up faster than it cools down.
- **Risk:** Can lead to serious health effects, reduced physical/mental ability, and increased risk of workplace incidents.
- **Responsibility:** Employers are responsible for managing heat stress risk in the workplace.

## Exposure Factors
- **Environment:** Direct/indirect sunlight, radiant heat from equipment (ovens, boilers), high air temperature, high humidity.
- **Work:** Physical exertion generating metabolic heat.
- **Worker:** Health conditions affecting heat response, dehydration, heat-trapping protective clothing.

## Signs & Symptoms
- **Early Warning:** Excess sweating, muscle cramps, weakness, fatigue, dizziness, nausea.
- **Severe/Heat Disorders:** Agitation, confusion, increased/irregular heart rate, decreased consciousness, seizures.
- **Consequence:** Untreated early signs can progress to heat exhaustion or heat stroke.

## Risk Assessment Methods
Assess potential for heat stress if workers are exposed to heat conditions.
- **Humidex Method**
- **Wet Bulb Globe Thermometer (WBGT) Method**
- **Body Temperature Measurement**
- **Heart Rate Measurement**
- **Guidance:** Use a heat stress screening tool to select the appropriate method based on workplace conditions.

## Control Measures (Hierarchy of Controls)
1.  **Elimination/Substitution:** Cancel/postpone work, move to cooler environment, replace strenuous work with less strenuous activities.
2.  **Engineering:** Install air conditioning, improve ventilation, insulate/cover hot surfaces, install shields/barriers, automate work, reduce humidity.
3.  **Administrative:** Reschedule work (avoid hottest times), implement work-rest cycles, rotate work between hot/cool environments, provide cool-down areas with access to cool water.
4.  **PPE:** Generally ineffective and can increase heat stress (e.g., heavy clothing). Heat-reflective or cooling suits may be appropriate in specific situations.

## Regulatory Guidance & Resources
- **Control Plan:** Required heat stress exposure control plan if workers are at risk.
- **Reference:** OHS Guidelines G7.29-5.
- **Tools:** Heat stress screening tool, publication on methods for measuring heat strain or stress, prevention booklet.
- **Additional:** Sun Safety at Work resources for outdoor workers.
