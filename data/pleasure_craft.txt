# Canadian Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC) & Safety Regulations

## Proof of Competency Requirements
- **Mandatory:** Required for operating motorized recreational pleasure crafts in Canada (includes electric trolling motors).
- **Required:** Even when motor is not in use (e.g., sailing).
- **Accepted Documents:**
  - Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC)
    - *Note:* A paper or electronic copy of a PCOC is NOT acceptable.
  - Certificate from Canadian course completed before April 1, 1999.
  - Professional marine certificate or equivalent (verified against Transport Canada list).
  - Completed Rental Boat Safety Checklist (valid for rental period only).
  - Visitor's home state/country operator card (if meeting requirements).
- **Exemptions:**
  - Waters of Nunavut or Northwest Territories.
  - Visitors operating their own craft for less than 45 consecutive days.

## Obtaining & Replacing PCOC
- **Issuance:** Pass a boating safety test delivered by accredited Transport Canada course providers.
- **Course Content:** Operator responsibilities, safety equipment, emergency procedures, waterway sharing, emergency response.
- **Validity:** Valid for life.
- **Replacement:** Lost/destroyed cards can be replaced from the course provider (fee applies).
- **Inquiries:** Contact Office of Boating Safety or use Course Provider Lookup tool.

## Youth Restrictions (Operators Under 16)
- **Under 12 Years (Unsupervised):** Max 10 hp / 7.5 kW.
- **Ages 12–15 Years (Unsupervised):** Max 40 hp / 30 kW.
- **Under 16 Years:** No Personal Watercraft (PWC) permitted.
- **Direct Supervision:** Requires a person 16+ years of age in the boat.
- **Territory Exceptions:** Restrictions do not apply in Nunavut or Northwest Territories.
