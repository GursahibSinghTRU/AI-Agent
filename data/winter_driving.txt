# Winter Driving Risk Mitigation Guidelines (BC Context)

## Overview
**Scope:** Winter and wet conditions in British Columbia.
**Objective:** Prevent accidents, vehicle breakdowns, and ensure compliance with BC winter tire laws.

## Vehicle Requirements & Maintenance
- **Tires:** Must meet BC winter tire laws (Mountain Peak and Snowflake symbol or M+S). Worn treads are hazardous.
- **Battery:** Cold weather weakens batteries; check before winter to prevent failure.
- **Pre-Winter Inspection:** Verify battery, wipers, lights, fluids, belts, hoses, and defrosters.
- **Tire Pressure:** Cold temperatures lower pressure; check frequently.
- **Snow Removal:** Clear roof, trunk, hood, and mirrors before driving to prevent projectiles on other vehicles.
- **Driveway:** Shovel snow promptly to prevent ice buildup and getting stranded.

## Safe Driving Protocols
- **Speed Control:** Drive for conditions, not the speed limit. Reduce speed to match traction capabilities.
- **Following Distance:** Maintain minimum of 4 car lengths (double normal distance) to allow reaction time.
- **Vehicle Systems:** ABS, ESC, and 4WD improve performance but are not foolproof against skidding.
- **Trip Necessity:** Minimize driving when possible. Combine errands and check road conditions via DriveBC.ca.
- **Inexperience:** Plan alternative transportation (transit, ride-share) if unseasoned in winter conditions.

## Pre-Drive Checklist
1. Defrost and clear all windows.
2. Remove snow and ice from roof/trunk/mirrors.
3. Verify tire pressure.
4. Review weather and street conditions.

## Emergency Response
- **Roadside Assistance:** Available 24/7 for stranded vehicles or battery failure.
- **Contact Numbers:** 1-888-268-2222 (BC), 1-800-222-4357 (North America).
- **Requirements:** Membership card number and photo ID required for assistance.
- **Alternative:** Mobile app available for faster dispatch.
