# Cold Stress and Outdoor Workers Safety
**Source:** British Columbia Medical Journal (BCMJ) / WorkSafeBC  
**Publication:** Nov 2024 (Vol. 66, No. 9)  
**Disclaimer:** WorkSafeBC opinion; not peer-reviewed by BCMJ Editorial Board.

## High-Risk Industries
- Construction
- Utilities
- Transport
- Emergency Response

## Environmental Risk Thresholds
- **Windchill:** Frostbite risk increases when windchill is below -27°C.
- **Factors:** Temperature, wind speed, and humidity combined.
- **Core Temperature:** Hypothermia defined as sustained core temperature below 35°C.

## Injury Management Protocols
### Frostbite
- **Symptoms:** Skin appears waxy, hard, numb; underlying tissues frozen.
- **Contraindications:** Do not rub, massage, or apply direct heat.
- **Treatment:** Use body heat or warm water (40–42°C) if refreezing can be avoided.
- **Warning:** Auto-amputation is a potential outcome.

### Hypothermia
- **Symptoms:** Shivering, confusion, difficulty walking/talking, unconsciousness, cardiac arrest.
- **Immediate Action:** Move indoors, obtain medical assistance.
- **Treatment:** Remove wet clothing; re-warm cautiously.
