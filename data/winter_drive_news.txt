# Winter Driving Safety Guidelines (B.C. Roads)

## Risk Indicators
- **Speed-Related Crashes:** Crashes due to driving too fast for conditions rise by 79% in December and 64% in January compared to October averages.
- **Pedestrian Incidents:** Nearly 50% of pedestrian crashes involving drivers occur between October and January.

## Vehicle Specifications
- **Tires:** Winter tires recommended below 7°C. Required on specific highways.
  - **Markings:** Look for Mountain Snowflake or M+S symbol.
  - **Tread Depth:** Minimum 3.5 mm.
- **Maintenance:** Check lights, wipers, and top up washer fluid before trips.

## Operational Safety Protocols
- **Speed Control:** Gears down; slow down for conditions. Avoid cruise control on slippery roads.
- **Maneuvering:** Increase following distance; avoid sudden acceleration, braking, or sharp turns.
- **Environment:** Check drivebc.ca for road conditions/closures. Allow extra travel time or avoid driving in poor conditions.
- **Visibility:** Keep phone out of sight; scan for pedestrians at intersections and transit stops.
- **Emergency Kit:** Include food, water, warm clothes, ice scraper, brush, shovel, and traction mats.

## Authority
- Safety advisories issued by ICBC and BC Highway Patrol.
