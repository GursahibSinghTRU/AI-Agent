# Avalanche Safety Plan: British Columbia Highway Maintenance

## Document Details
- **Authority:** Province of British Columbia (Highway & Bridge Maintenance)
- **Season:** November through May (Mountain snowpack assessment)
- **Primary Audience:** Maintenance contractors, workers, and motorists

## Operational Objectives
- Ensure safety of all highway users and maintenance workers.
- Minimize frequency and duration of avalanche-related road closures.

## Key Resources & Documentation
- **Safety Plan:** Core practices for working in highway operations within avalanche terrain.
- **Maps & Atlases:** Avalanche Area Codes, Atlases, and Program-specific maps.
- **Threshold Guidelines:** Safety measure thresholds (Updated Jan 2026).
- **Maintenance Specifications:** Schedule 1 Section 3 (Winter Maintenance); 3.04 (Snow Avalanche Response).
- **Risk Assessment:** Backcountry Avalanche Risk Assessment Protocol (Updated Feb 2026).
- **Emergency Response:** Highway Avalanche Incident Response Plans, Resource Lists, and Vehicle Inserts.

## Personnel Roles
- District Avalanche Technician
- District Avalanche Supervisor
- District Avalanche Assistant
- Senior Avalanche Officer
- Incident Commander & Task Force Team Leader

## Contact & Inquiry
- **Email:** TTWebmaster@gov.bc.ca
- **Use:** Questions, comments, or suggestions regarding working in avalanche areas.
