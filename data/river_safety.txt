# Safety Guidelines: River & Wildlife (Metro Vancouver)

## River Safety Protocols
- **Trails:** Stay on designated trails and follow all posted signs.
- **Water Access:** Do not enter the river. Risks include hypothermia, unexpected surges, fast currents, and debris (rocks/branches).
- **Monitoring:** Continuously monitor water levels; avoid areas marked as dangerous.

## Visitor Preparedness
- **Awareness:** Maintain situational awareness; avoid distractions (e.g., headphones).
- **Companionship:** Do not hike alone; bring a friend.
- **Communication:** Inform others of location and expected return time. Keep mobile phone on for rescue tracking.
- **Gear:** Bring water, snacks, flashlight, and appropriate clothing/footwear.
- **Timing:** Know personal limits; allow sufficient time to return before dark.
- **If Lost:** Stay in place, increase visibility, and wait for help.
- **Park Status:** Do not use parks when closed to the public.

## Wildlife Safety (Bear Encounters)
- **General Conduct:** Do not feed wildlife or leave food/garbage. Maintain a safe distance.
- **Bear Encounter Response:**
  - Remain calm and speak in a non-threatening voice.
  - Back away slowly.
  - Avoid direct eye contact, shouting, or throwing objects.
- **Trail Etiquette:** Warn animals of presence by talking, clapping, or making noise on low-traffic trails.
