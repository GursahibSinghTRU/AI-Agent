# Hiking Safety & Risk Mitigation Guidelines

## Core Responsibilities
*   **Self-Sufficiency:** Individuals are responsible for their own safety. Maintain self-reliance during emergencies.
*   **Emergency Protocols:** Verify emergency protocols with Parks Canada or the destination site prior to departure.

## Pre-Trip Planning
*   **Trail Assessment:** Select trails matching group experience and physical ability.
    *   *Ratings:* Easy (Green Circle), Moderate (Blue Square), Difficult (Black Diamond), Route (Two Black Diamonds).
*   **Pacing:** Estimate travel time based on beginner speeds (2–2.5 km/hr). Avoid trails with significant elevation gain for novices.
*   **Conditions:** Review current trail conditions and weather forecasts.
*   **Trip Plan:** Inform a third party of the route, expected return time, and emergency contacts.
*   **Communication:** Do not rely solely on phone GPS due to limited cell service in nature areas.

## Gear & Equipment
*   **Safety Kit:** Pack a first aid kit, multi-tool, flashlight, fire starter, whistle, and navigation tools (map, compass, GPS).
*   **Footwear:** Wear shoes with support, grip, and protection. Keep feet dry; avoid flip-flops.
*   **Clothing:** Use lightweight, breathable layers for weather changes. Include sun protection (sunglasses, sunscreen, hat).

## Sustenance & Hydration
*   **Water:** Plan for ~0.5 liters per hour. Do not wait until thirsty to hydrate.
*   **Food:** Carry nutritious, high-energy snacks. Pack complete meals for hikes exceeding a few hours.

## Environmental & Wildlife Safety
*   **Wildlife Distancing:**
    *   100 meters (10 bus lengths) from bears, bison, cougars, and wolves.
    *   30 meters (3 bus lengths) from elk, moose, deer, sheep, and goats.
    *   Never feed, touch, or approach wildlife.
*   **Trail Etiquette:** Stay on marked trails to protect vegetation and wildlife.
*   **Waste Management:** Use designated bins or pack out trash/recyclables. Practice Leave No Trace principles.
