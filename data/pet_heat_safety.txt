# Summer Pet Safety & Heat Risk Guidelines

## Critical Hazards
- **Vehicle Exposure:** Leaving pets in a parked vehicle on a hot day is lethal; death can occur within 10 minutes. Never leave pets unattended in vehicles.
- **Brachycephalic Breeds:** Dogs with shortened noses (bulldogs, boxers, pugs, shih tzus) are highly susceptible to overheating due to compromised panting ability.
- **Toxic Substances:** Monitor pets at gatherings for ingestion of raisins, grapes, onions, chocolate, and xylitol-containing products. Alcohol exposure causes intoxication and coma.

## Home Environment Safety
- **Windows:** Ensure open windows have secure, locking screens to prevent escape or accidental falls.
- **Comfort Zones:** Maintain access to cooler areas (e.g., basement, bathrooms). Place crates in cool rooms.
- **Hydration:** Provide an ample, continuous supply of fresh water.
- **Cooling:** Use cooling mats inside crates; mats activate via body pressure.
- **Identification:** Keep ID tags updated. Label indoor cats as 'indoor cat' to prevent unauthorized wandering.

## Outdoor Activity Protocols
- **Timing:** Schedule exercise during early morning or late evening to avoid midday heat.
- **Cooling:** Utilize dog-friendly recreational water areas to lower body temperature.
- **Heatstroke Indicators:** Monitor for distressed breathing, labored panting, vomiting, glazed eyes, difficulty walking, or constant snorting.

## Grooming Guidelines
- **Shaving:** Shaving fur can increase risk by removing insulating properties and sun protection.
- **Recommendation:** Opt for trimming and regular brushing to improve air circulation. Consult a veterinarian for breed-specific grooming advice.
