# Winter Sports Safety Guidelines
**Child Injury Prevention Alliance Reference**

## Common Hazards & Risks
- **Injury Types:** Cuts, bruises, broken bones, and brain injuries from falls or crashes.
- **Sled Speeds:** Can reach 25 miles per hour.
- **Ice Hazards:** Thin patches on frozen bodies of water can break.
- **Environment:** Avoid sledding near trees, fences, ponds, light poles, or streets.

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- **Clothing:** Warm winter coat, hat, gloves, and slip-resistant snow boots.
- **Helmets:** Mandatory for skiing, snowboarding, and snowmobiling. Multi-sport or bicycle helmets acceptable for ice skating or sledding if a ski helmet is unavailable.
- **Additional Gear:** 
  - Goggles (skiing, snowboarding, snowmobiling).
  - Knee and elbow pads (ice skating).
  - Wrist guards with gloves (snowboarding).

## Age Recommendations (American Academy of Pediatrics)
- **Sledding:** Children under 5 years must sled with an adult.
- **Snowboarding:** Recommended for children 7 years or older.
- **Snowmobiling:**
  - Ride as passenger: 6 years or older.
  - Operate vehicle: 16 years or older.

## Safety Rules
- **Sledding:**
  - One person per sled unless an adult is supervising a young child.
  - Always sit upright and face forward.
  - Never sled head first.
  - Prefer steerable sleds over snow disks or inner tubes.
  - Never ride while being pulled by a motorized vehicle (car, ATV, snowmobile).
- **Ice Skating:** Use designated areas only. Avoid river ice or thawed/refrozen ice.
