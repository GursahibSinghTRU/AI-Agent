# Outdoor Winter Safety Guidelines (Risk & Safety Knowledge Base)

## General Conditions & Weather
*   **Forecast Check:** Always review weather forecasts before outdoor activities.
*   **Temperature Limits:**
    *   **Stay Indoors:** When temperature (with/without wind chill) is below -27°C (-16°F).
    *   **Limit Time:** When temperature is below -15°C (5°F).
*   **Hydration & Sun Protection:** Drink warm fluids (or water) to prevent dehydration; use sunscreen even on cloudy days.
*   **Vigilance:** Monitor for shivering or signs of hypothermia/frostbite. Children cannot communicate discomfort effectively.

## Ice Thickness Standards
*   **Walking/Individuals:** Minimum 15 cm (6 in.).
*   **Groups:** Minimum 20 cm (8 in.).
*   **Sleds/Snowmobiles:** Minimum 25 cm (10 in.).
*   **Ice Integrity:**
    *   **Safe:** Clear blue ice (strongest).
    *   **Caution:** White opaque/snow ice (half strength of blue).
    *   **Unsafe:** Grey ice.
*   **Hazards:** Avoid areas under dams (downstream weakening), near banks during spring thaw, or where surface snow blankets ice (insulation effect).

## Clothing & Gear
*   **Layering System:**
    1.  **Base:** Thin moisture-wicking material (e.g., polyester).
    2.  **Mid:** Insulating layer (wool or fleece).
    3.  **Outer:** Waterproof/windproof layer (nylon or Gore-Tex).
*   **Accoutrements:** Include hat, mitts, tube scarf, and waterproof boots.
*   **Ski/Snowboard Bindings:** Check annually by a qualified technician; ensure proper fit.
*   **Boots:** Skates should give ankle support and fit snugly.
*   **Helmets:**
    *   **Required:** Skating, Skiing, Snowboarding.
    *   **Standard:** CSA stamped for Canada; replace every 5 years.
    *   **Sledding:** Recommended (Ski helmet preferred over bicycle helmet).

## Activity-Specific Safety Rules
### Skiing & Snowboarding
*   **Ages:** Snowboarding not recommended for children under 7 years.
*   **Supervision:** Always use the buddy system; never alone.
*   **Hazards:** Watch for other skiers, obstacles, rocks, and changing visibility.
*   **Timing:** Quit before getting tired or dark; do not ski after using drugs/alcohol.

### Tobogganing & Sledding
*   **Supervision:** Young children must be supervised by an adult; never toboggan alone or at night.
*   **Positioning:** Safest is kneeling. Avoid stomach (head) first or lying flat on back (spine risk).
*   **Equipment:**
    *   Do not use inner tubes or plastic discs (hard to control).
    *   Avoid sleds with steering wheels (unstable).
    *   Ensure no loose clothing or long scarves.
*   **Motorized Vehicles:** **NEVER** pull children on a toboggan by motorized vehicles (ORVs, snowmobiles); collision risk if vehicle stops abruptly.

### Snowmobiling
*   **Rider Age:** Children under 16 should not drive.
*   **Passenger Age:** Children under 6 should not ride as passengers.
*   **PPE:** Every rider must wear a snowmobile helmet.
*   **Training:** Drivers must receive instruction from an instructor/association.
*   **Prohibitions:** Do not tow persons behind snowmobiles. Avoid road/rail crossings without caution.
