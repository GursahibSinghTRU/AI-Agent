# Hiking Safety Guidelines (BC RCMP)
## Preparation & Planning
- **Route Knowledge:** Study maps, guidebooks, and local weather forecasts before departure.
- **Emergency Contact:** Always leave destination and schedule details with a trusted contact.
- **Group Rule:** Never hike alone in forests, canyons, or hazardous areas. Partners provide assistance, morale, and help if one is injured.

## Gear & Attire
- **Clothing:** Layered clothing for changing conditions. Avoid cotton when wet/cold as it insulates poorly.
- **Footwear:** Appropriate hiking shoes or boots are mandatory.
- **Day Pack Contents:** Rain gear, extra warm layers, high-energy food, water, first aid kit, pocket knife, waterproof matches, insect repellent.
- **Navigation:** Carry a flashlight/pen light and a GPS unit.

## Emergency Protocols
- **If Lost:** Remain calm, stay dry, stay warm, and stay put.
- **Overnight Stuck:** Build a campfire for warmth and smoke signaling (aids search aircraft). Construct a small shelter from dead branches/boughs if weather is severe.
- **Exit Strategy:** If unsure, follow a stream downhill to reach inhabited areas.

## Search and Rescue (SAR) Context
- **Volume:** BC SAR responds to nearly 1000 incidents/year.
- **Efficiency:** 95% of missing/injured subjects located within 24 hours of activation.
- **Volunteers:** 2500 unpaid professionals across 80+ communities donate over 100,000 hours annually.

**Source:** Royal Canadian Mounted Police (BC Section)  
**Last Updated:** 2024-08-27
