# TRU Onboarding & Key Policies Guide

Welcome to Thompson Rivers University! Whether you're joining us as a faculty member or staff, your contributions are essential to our vibrant and inclusive community.

## Welcome Message

At TRU, we believe that a great start leads to a great journey. People and Culture is here to support you every step of the way. From your first day to your first semester and beyond, we're committed to helping you feel informed, connected, and empowered.

We've created a comprehensive onboarding experience to guide you through key steps, resources, and opportunities.

This is a flexible resource—no quizzes, no deadlines. Explore at your own pace and revisit anytime.

## Key Policies & Procedures

These documents help ensure a safe, respectful, and transparent workplace for everyone. You don't need to memorize them—but it's helpful to know where to find them and what they cover.

## New Employee Onboarding Checklist - For Managers

### Before Start Date

- Ensure the new employee does not start employment without a signed contract returned to People & Culture
- Ensure the new employee has completed all hiring paperwork and (if applicable) benefits information with People & Culture
- Call the new employee to confirm the finalization of the process, officially welcoming them to TRU and the department
- Provide them with the name, phone number and email of any other required points of contact
- Discuss what they can expect from their first few weeks at TRU:
  - Time they should arrive
  - Location of the office
  - Where to park
  - What to bring
  - Office attire
- Create a department orientation schedule to arrange introductions and tours
- Send an announcement to your team and colleagues introducing the new employee and advising their start date
- Discuss training roles and responsibilities with required team members
- Prepare the new employee's workspace:
  - Ensure space is clean and cleared
  - Remove past incumbent's belongings
  - Make it a welcoming space equipped with required materials
- Make arrangements for the new employee's name on the office door
- Submit an ASAR via the IT Services Portal
- Email the IT Service Desk to request telephone/voicemail setup
- Order business cards by emailing Print Services (if needed)
- Confirm inclusion to relevant email distribution lists
- Confirm access to departmental Outlook mailboxes and appropriate electronic files
- Prepare the TRU Key Requisition Form (if employee requires a key)

### First Day & Week

- Welcome your new team member to TRU and the department
- Take time to relate and show empathy towards starting something new; share your own TRU journey
- Show them their workstation/office
- Introduce them to team/department members
- Briefly describe department structure, organization and priorities and remind the employee how they will contribute to departmental outcomes
- Confirm parking is sorted and offer assistance or refer to TRU's Transportation resources
- Discuss building/office access and alarm system
- Tour meeting rooms, washrooms, lunchrooms, designated smoking areas, etc.
- Take the new employee on a tour of campus
- Introduce them to colleagues and other departments they may need to interact with
- Provide login instructions and where to access electronic files
- Review OneTRU and myTRU including timesheet reporting
- Review standard voicemail and email greetings/signatures

Welcome again, and we're so glad you're here!

- Unsafe acts and conditions are anticipated and corrected before harm is done

# Risk & Safety Training & Orientation Resources

Training and orientation at work is one key to staying safe at TRU.

## Workplace Orientation

All staff require specific orientation to the hazards they may encounter in their daily work environment. In-person workplace orientation is the responsibility of department leadership, including deans, chairs, managers, supervisors, and senior staff with supervisory roles.

For the complete onboarding process, visit People & Culture's SharePoint.

## Online Learning Courses

TRU has created online learning courses to support safety in classrooms and workplaces. Available courses include:
- Arts Carpentry Studio Orientation
- Chemistry Laboratory Safety
- WHMIS Study Guide

Contact safety@tru.ca if you or your team need assistance developing custom safety courses.

## Health & Safety Course (Deltek/HRSmart)

TRU has created a course on Deltek/HRSmart for new employees that provides a detailed look at health and safety. This course is part of the onboarding package from People & Culture. It is recommended that all TRU staff review the Health and Safety at TRU course on an annual basis.

## WHMIS Training

TRU employees, students, and teaching assistants who work where hazardous products are used, stored, or handled must successfully complete WHMIS training. Training is free and available to students on Moodle.

An online WHMIS training module is available to TRU employees through Deltek/HRSmart (requires TRU email address and/or TRU ID).

## Back Injury Prevention

A back injury can be one of the most disabling injuries and life-altering injuries, with significant impact on daily activities. This course outlines ways to help prevent back injuries from occurring, both at work and at home.

## Ergonomics & Desk Safety

While desk work may not be considered strenuous physical activity, it can still take a toll on the body. Extended periods of sitting, long screen time sessions, awkward neck positions, and repetitive finger movements can result in pain, strain, and chronic injury.

Learn how to avoid these common problems with good posture and ergonomics. The good news is you don't need a lot of time, space, or money to set up a safe and comfortable work space—at home or in the office.

### Deskercise Tips

Logging long hours seated at the computer takes a toll, including short- and long-term pain, injury, and increased disease risk. Short stretches and exercises spaced throughout the workday can help. A few minutes every hour offer many benefits:
- Increased mobility and muscle control
- Less stress and pain
- Decreased risk of sedentary-related diseases (chronic back pain, cardiovascular issues, type 2 diabetes)

## Transportation of Dangerous Goods (TDG)

Anyone who imports, offers for transport, handles or transports dangerous goods are required by law to complete a TDG course every three years. This is typically required for material distribution teams and select science staff.

An online course can be completed within three hours. To request access:
- Send a request email to safety@tru.ca
- They will provide course access information
- Cost is covered by the worker's department

## JOHSC Training

All members of the Joint Occupational Health and Safety Committee (JOHSC) are required to participate in 8 hours of training upon joining the committee and are entitled to 8 hours of safety training per year afterwards.

Please contact safety@tru.ca for more information and visit WorkSafeBC.

## Emergency Guidelines

Community Safety and Emergency Management SharePoint has guidelines and resources for how the TRU community should respond to emergency situations including:
- Active threat and lockdown
- Evacuations
- Fire and fire extinguisher basics

Contact safety@tru.ca or risk@tru.ca if you have any questions or concerns.

For written safe work procedures, visit our Safe Work Procedures page.

# TRU Safety Alerts & Emergency Response

## TRU SAFE App

The free TRU SAFE app provides just-in-time information on safety, security and emergency situations. It is TRU's official platform for emergency notifications.

### TRU Safe App Features

**ALERTS** - Receive notifications about potential dangers or situations on or near campus that could affect your health or safety.

**CONTACT** - Easily contact:
- Security
- Emergency services
- First aid
- Access to variety of campus maps
- Security/safety guides
- Emergency response information

**FRIEND WALK** - Send your location to a friend so they can watch you walk to your destination in real time. They can trigger a call to emergency services if necessary.

**RESOURCES** - Relevant safety information for the TRU community.

### Download the App

TRU students, employees and family members are strongly encouraged to download the official safety app (free) from your Apple or Android app store—it's the way to ensure you are informed on all things safety.

## TRU Alerts - Emergency Notifications

TRU Alerts relays key information in a timely manner to ensure the safety of TRU students and employees while keeping the community, including parents, informed.

### Sign Up for TRU Alerts

Sign-up is compulsory and required for:
- Email notifications
- Text messages (SMS)
- Phone calls to your cell phone

Visit our public website to sign up for TRU Alerts and for more information.

## Emergency Response Procedures

Should you be directly involved or witness an incident, get help and notify your leadership (supervisor, manager, dean, instructor) as soon as possible.

### If You Experience or Witness an Incident:

**1. Assess Situation**
- Prioritize injury
- Determine if you need security/first aid or 9-1-1 (ambulance, police, fire)
- Move to a safe area if needed

**2. Call for Help**

**Kamloops Campus:**
- Security/First Aid: 250-828-5033

**Williams Lake Campus:**
- First Aid: 250-392-8000
- Security/First Aid: 250-214-9610 (after hours)

**Emergency Services:**
- Call 9-1-1 for ambulance, police, or fire

**3. Provide Details**

Tell security/first aid/9-1-1 your:
- Location
- Situation and severity
- Contact information

**4. Report the Incident**
- Notify your supervisor/instructor
- Report the incident to Risk and Safety Services (online report form)
- Help with follow-up and action items
