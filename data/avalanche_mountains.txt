# Avalanche Safety Knowledge Base Entry

## Overview
- **Threat Definition:** Rapid surge of compact snow, rock, and debris. Speed often exceeds 100 km/h.
- **Primary Context:** Winter mountain activities, specifically off-piste/freeriding.
- **Critical Safety Note:** Off-piste travel should only be conducted with a professional guide trained in mountain safety.

## Risk Indicators & Warning Signs
- **Terrain:** Steep slopes and areas with recently accumulated snow.
- **Snow Conditions:** Fresh snow buildup, unstable accumulations, and rapid temperature changes.
- **Visual/Auditory Cues:** Cracks in snow, thumping noises, and breaking snow surfaces.
- **Trigger Factors:** Weight accumulation, temperature shifts, vibrations, or external movement.

## Immediate Action During Avalanche
- **Evasion:** Move perpendicular to the avalanche path; move above the fracture line if underfoot.
- **Face Protection:** Keep face above snow to maintain breathing and reduce suffocation risk.
- **Surface Mobility:** Swim towards the surface to avoid sinking; thrash arms if necessary.
- **Visibility:** Raise one arm to assist rescue teams.
- **Breathing:** Create air space in front of face; maintain calm breathing to prevent CO2 buildup.
- **Post-Avalanche:** Free yourself from snow immediately upon stopping. Seek medical help for hypothermia.

## Essential Equipment
- **Avalanche Victim Locator (AVL):** Electromagnetic device for locating buried victims.
- **Probe and Shovel:** Required for searching and freeing victims.

## Prevention & Management
- **Information:** Consult daily avalanche bulletins from reliable sources (e.g., Avalanche-Report).
- **Resort Measures:** Follow local authority checks, rules, and controlled avalanche triggering.
- **Guidance:** Use experienced guides for terrain assessment and risk evaluation.
- **Emergency Contact:** Use local mountain rescue telephone numbers (e.g., 112).
