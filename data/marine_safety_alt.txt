# Boating Safety Guidelines (Transport Canada)

## Regulatory Authority
- **Office of Boating Safety**: Oversees regulations, standards, enforcement, and technical services for recreational vessels.
- **Responsibility**: Operator is responsible for the safety of guests and others using the waterway.

## Mandatory Compliance
- **Pleasure Craft License**: Required application before purchase.
- **Operator Certification**: Valid Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC) mandatory.
  - *Replacement*: Use Course Provider Lookup tool for lost/destroyed cards.
- **Restractions**: Adherence to Vessel Operation Restriction Regulations.

## Operational Safety Protocols
- **Impairment**: Zero tolerance for alcohol and other substances while operating a vessel.
- **Safety Gear**: Proper selection of Personal Floatation Devices (PFDs) for adults and children.
- **Pre-Departure Checks**:
  - Verify water and weather conditions.
  - Prepare trip plans.
  - Conduct courtesy and pre-departure inspections.
- **Speed**: Manage speed appropriately for conditions and safety.

## Resources & Alerts
- **Mobile Tool**: Discover Boating app (rules, guidelines, best practices).
- **Information**: Marine Safety Publications (TPs), recall notices, and flood navigation warnings.
- **Liability**: Government of Canada not responsible for accuracy or currency of external source information.
- **Contact**: Office of Boating Safety for concerns or feedback.
