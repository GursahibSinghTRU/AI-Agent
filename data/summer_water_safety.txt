# Water Safety Risk Factors & Regulations (RCMP Guidelines)
**Source:** Royal Canadian Mounted Police (BC) | **Date Modified:** 2024-08-23
**Scope:** Summer Water Safety, Boating, Swimming Operations

## Statistical Risk Factors (BC Coroners Service 2008-2012)
- **Seasonality:** 58.7% of drowning deaths occur between May and August.
- **Demographics:** 81.1% of drowning victims were male; highest risk age group 20-29 years.
- **Contributing Factors:** Alcohol/drugs were contributing factors in 40.2% of drowning deaths.
- **Incident Distribution:** 397 deaths total (200 recreational activities, 175 falls/vehicle water incidents, 220 occupational).

## Critical Safety Protocols
- **PFDs:** Mandatory use of personal flotation devices. Do not assume time to put on a lifejacket; tragedy can strike instantly.
- **Alcohol Policy:** Impaired driving laws apply strictly to boat operation. Never operate watercraft under the influence.
- **Swimming:** Non-swimmers must remain out of the water and seek shade.
- **Current Management:** Avoid placing feet in fast-moving water deeper than arm length (entanglement/face-down submersion risk).
- **Speed:** Reduce speed on waterways and roadways leading to them to prevent incidents.
- **Planning:** Inform others of destination and expected return time when visiting less-traveled areas.

## Regulatory Requirements (Power-Driven Craft)
- **Proof of Competency:** Required for all operators. Acceptable proofs include:
    - Pleasure Craft Operator Card
    - Course completed before April 1, 1999
    - Specified marine certificate (List of Certificates of Competency)
    - Completed rental-boat safety checklist (rental period only)
- **Applicability:** Safety equipment rules apply to all watercraft types (canoes to yachts).
- **Enforcement:** RCMP West Coast Marine Services conduct patrols for regulation compliance.
