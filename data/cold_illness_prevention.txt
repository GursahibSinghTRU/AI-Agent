# Safety Summary: Preventing Cold-Related Illness
**Target Audience:** TRU Students in Motion Picture/Film Production
**Document Source:** ACTSAFE SAFETY BULLETIN #341

## 1. Hazard Overview
Working in cold, wet, or windy conditions poses severe risks to performers and crew members.
*   **Potential Consequences:** Cold-related illness can lead to permanent tissue damage, gangrene, amputation, or death.
*   **When it Occurs:** Illness can happen at any time of year, particularly with prolonged exposure.
*   **High-Risk Scenarios:** Risks increase during practices such as:
    *   Working near manufactured wind or rain towers.
    *   Wearing costumes inappropriate for the temperature conditions.
    *   Emergency situations involving water submersion (e.g., falling into water).
*   **Physiology:** Illness (cold stress) occurs when skin temperature drops and the body cannot warm itself. Normal body temperature is ~37°C.

## 2. Responsibilities
*   **Employers:** Must maintain an overall health and safety program specific to the workplace.
*   **Workers & Supervisors:** Must recognize individual risk factors and understand signs of cold stress.

## 3. Individual Risk Factors
Workers should assess personal risk before starting in cold environments. Factors include:
*   Underlying health conditions.
*   Poor physical conditioning.
*   Previous cold injuries.
*   Fatigue.
*   Medications.
*   Age (specifically for children/elderly).

## 4. Signs of Cold-Related Illness

| Condition | Description | Key Symptoms |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Chilblains** | Painful, non-freezing injury (hands/feet). <br> *Risk:* Infection/severe damage if untreated. | • Small, red, swollen skin areas<br>• Tingling, burning, itching<br>• Tender blue bumps (after rewarming) |
| **Trench Foot** | Non-freezing injury of feet caused by wet/cold exposure. | • Tingling, burning, then numbness<br>• Heavy or "wooden" feet; difficulty walking<br>• Red, swollen skin that turns pale and clammy |
| **Frostbite** | Serious condition where skin/tissues freeze (extremities). <br> *Risk:* Gangrene, amputation, permanent damage. | • Pins and needles, throbbing<br>• Skin turns pale/blue; feels hard/frozen<br>• Clumsiness from joint stiffness<br>• Painful blisters/scabs upon rewarming |
| **Hypothermia** | **Medical Emergency:** Body temp falls below 35ºC. <br>Can occur in warmer temps if chilled by water/sweat. | • Shivering stops (once moderate/severe)<br>• Clumsiness, lack of coordination<br>• Drowsiness, slurred speech, confusion<br>• Slow, shallow breathing; weak pulse<br>• Loss of consciousness |

## 5. Emergency Response
If hypothermia is suspected or a severe cold injury occurs:
1.  Contact **First Aid**.
2.  Call **911**.
