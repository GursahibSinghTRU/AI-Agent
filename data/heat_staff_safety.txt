# Safety Knowledge Base: Extreme Heat & Staff Safety

## Source
SafeCare BC Resources & Safety Huddles

## Objective
Protect staff and residents/clients in healthcare settings from heat stress and extreme weather hazards.

## Safety Huddle Protocol
- **Pre-Meeting:** Review organization's written safety procedures (Exposure Control Plan/Emergency Preparedness). Print safety posters for distribution.
- **During Meeting:** Outline risk factors for heat exhaustion and heat stroke. Review preventative measures and site-specific protocols.
- **Post-Meeting:** Display safety posters at site to reinforce training.

## Heat Stress Management
- **Signs:** Staff must recognize and report signs of heat exhaustion and heat stroke immediately.
- **PPE:** Full PPE (goggles, masks, gowns) may become unsafe in extreme heat. Consult health authority for updated guidance.
- **Breaks:** Implement micro-breaks for staff wearing full PPE. Provide additional masks to replace soiled/damp units.
- **Scheduling:** Move procedures to cooler rooms or earlier in the day to reduce thermal exposure.
- **Refusal:** Follow established processes to report concerns and refuse unsafe work.

## Vulnerable Populations & Environment
- **Residents:** Prioritize safety for frail residents, those on oxygen, and individuals with mobility challenges.
- **Air Quality:** Monitor for air quality advisories during wildfires or smoke events (e.g., Air Quality Health Index 10+).

## Documentation & Regulations
- **Required Plans:** Maintain Exposure Control Plan for communicable diseases and extreme heat.
- **Regulatory Updates:** Note amendments to OHSR Part 5 (Chemical Agents and Biological Agents – Emergency Planning effective Feb 3, 2025).

## Related Tools & Resources
- **Posters:** "Summer is here (and so are its hazards)".
- **Templates:** Exposure Control Plan Template.
- **Safety Huddles:** Environmental scan, Refusal of unsafe work, Environmental cleaning, De-escalation, Debriefing critical incidents.
- **Webinars:** Smart nutrition for sustained energy, Fatigue to focus.
