# Summer Water Safety Knowledge Summary

### **High-Risk Demographics & Statistics**
*   **Most Vulnerable:** Drowning is a leading cause of unintentional death for children ages 1–4.
*   **At-Risk Groups:** 
    *   Children aged 1–4
    *   Men aged 15–44
*   **The Reality:** Children can drown in very shallow water (a few centimetres). A small child can disappear and drown in seconds.
*   **Near Drowning:** For every death, an estimated 4–5 near-drownings occur, often resulting in hospitalization and potential brain damage.

### **Active Supervision Rules**
*   **Constant Watch:** Always actively watch children near water (pools, bathtubs, beaches, water parks).
*   **No Distractions:** Never leave a child unattended in the bathtub to answer the phone or perform any other task.
*   **Life Jackets:** Require all non-swimmers to wear life jackets to keep them at the surface while supervising.
*   **Child Supervision:** Children should never be relied upon to supervise other children.

### **Backyard Pool Safety**
*   **Barriers:** Ensure adequate barriers are in place to prevent unsupervised access.
    *   Fencing: At least 1.2 m in height.
    *   Gaps: No larger than 10 cm.
    *   Gates: Must be self-closing and self-latching.
*   **Portable Pools:** Empty toddler pools immediately after each use to prevent access.

### **General Water Safety Principles**
*   **Skills vs. Safety:** Swimming skills alone are not enough. Knowledge of emergency preparation and response is essential.
*   **Adult Factors:** Alcohol consumption and water currents significantly increase the risk of adult fatalities.
*   **Location Awareness:** 
    *   Infants/Toddlers typically drown in bathtubs or small pools.
    *   Older children/youth typically drown in larger bodies of water.
*   **Diving:** Head-first diving should be avoided unless the depth and conditions are appropriate.

### **Key Takeaway**
Combining swimming skills with water safety knowledge—including proper supervision and environmental preparation—is the most effective way to prevent fatalities and near-drownings.
