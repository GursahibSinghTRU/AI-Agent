# Winter Driving Safety Guidelines (British Columbia)

## Regulatory Requirements
- **Enforcement Period:** Mandatory winter tire and chain rules apply from **October 1 to April 30**. For select highways not in mountain passes or high snowfall areas, the period ends **March 31**.
- **Signage:** Requirements are marked by roadside signs.
- **Enforcement:** Agencies including RCMP and CVSE may turn vehicles around and issue fines if drivers proceed beyond signs without proper equipment.

## Vehicle Readiness & Visibility
- **Lighting:** Always turn exterior lights fully on (headlights and tail lights). Do not rely solely on Daytime Running Lights (DRLs) or interior dashboard indicators.
- **Preparation:** Ensure vehicle is winter-ready and carry appropriate clothing and emergency supplies.
- **Strategy:** The safest option is to avoid driving until weather improves.

## Resources & Contacts
- **Highway Conditions:** Monitor **DriveBC** for real-time highway conditions and weather forecasts.
- **Guides:** Refer to the "Shift into Winter" guide for comprehensive safety information.
- **Emergency Reporting:** Use the BC conditions hotline (**1-800-550-4997**) or report highway problems online.
- **Avalanche Areas:** Be aware of designated zones and consult relevant guides before travel.
