# Winter Power Sports Safety Guidelines
**Source:** Canadian Boilermakers (2021)
**Context:** Winter recreational vehicle safety (snowmobiles, sleds)

## General Operational Safety
- **Speed:** Maintain realistic speeds to reduce accident risk and injury.
- **Tethers:** Use safety tethers that shut off the motor upon separation. Mandatory for sledding.
- **Ice Safety:** Verify ice depth and strength before crossing. Stagger snowmobiles while crossing frozen water. Avoid gray or slushy ice.
- **Buddy System:** Never ride alone. Stay together regardless of skill level.
- **Skill Matching:** Ensure trip plans match individual abilities. Do not assign high-horsepower machines to young or inexperienced operators.
- **PPE:** Always wear a helmet and dress appropriately for weather conditions.
- **Sobriety:** Operating a snow machine while impaired carries penalties equivalent to motor vehicle offenses.
- **Emergency Kit:** Carry first aid supplies, signaling flares, and fire-starting materials.

## Avalanche Country Protocols
- **Gear:** Every rider must carry and know how to use an avalanche transceiver, shovel, and probe.
- **Training:** Complete an avalanche safety course before entering backcountry terrain.
- **Forecast:** Plan routes based on current avalanche and weather forecasts.
- **Terrain Awareness:** Recent avalanche activity indicates unstable snow. Avoid riding on or underneath slopes.
- **Group Protocol:**
  - Pass avalanche zones one at a time.
  - Do not group up in runout zones.
  - Do not rush to rescue a stuck friend; wait for the team to arrive or call professionals.
