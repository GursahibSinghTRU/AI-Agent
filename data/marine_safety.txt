# Boating Safety Standards & Guidelines (Transport Canada)

## Regulatory Requirements
- **Pleasure Craft License:** Mandatory application required before purchasing a pleasure craft.
- **Operator Certification:** A Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC) is required for operation.
- **Vessel Classification:** Distinguish between pleasure craft and non-pleasure craft to determine applicable regulations.
- **Vessel Operation Restrictions:** Apply for restrictions where applicable (e.g., seasonal or location-based limits).

## Safety Protocols & Best Practices
- **Equipment:** Must carry appropriate Personal Floatation Devices (PFDs) for adults and children.
- **Pre-Departure:** Verify weather and water conditions; complete pre-departure checklists and courtesy checks.
- **Impairment:** Zero tolerance for alcohol or substances; operators must remain alert.
- **Trip Planning:** Development of trip plans is recommended for safety.
- **Navigation:** Use the *Discover Boating* app for access to rules, guidelines, and alerts.

## Risk Management & Resources
- **Recalls & Alerts:** Monitor Transport Canada for product recalls and safety notices affecting equipment.
- **External Sources:** Content from third-party sources is not subject to official language, privacy, or accessibility requirements; Government of Canada does not guarantee accuracy of external information.
- **Enforcement:** Reference CSA 2001 sources for police enforcement authorities and acceptance of electronic distress signals.
- **Support:** Contact the Office of Boating Safety for concerns, complaints, or licensing assistance.
- **Related Topics:** Flood condition navigation, abandoned boats/wrecks, and marine safety equipment initiatives.
