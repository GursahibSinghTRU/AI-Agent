# Safety Summary: Working on Ice-Covered Water

### ⚠️ Key Hazards & Risks
*   **Primary Risks:** Falling through ice can result in drowning, severe injury, or hypothermia.
*   **Common Hazard Sources:**
    *   Damaged or compromised ice strength.
    *   Overloading the ice sheet.
    *   Working over areas of thin ice.
    *   Exposure to cold temperatures.

### 🧊 Ice Characteristics & Strength
*   **Ice Quality Matters:**
    *   **Strong:** Solid, clear, blue ice (no air trapped).
    *   **Weak:** White, opaque "snow ice" (contains trapped air).
*   **Strength Variables:** Ice integrity is affected by ice quality, color, thickness, cracks, air/water temperatures, wind, water movement, and snow cover.
*   **Freezing Points:** Fresh water freezes at 0°C; salt water freezes at approximately -2°C.
*   **Visual Inspection:** Monitor for cracks (dry or wet), snowbanks, and signs of thermal contraction/expansion.

### 🛡️ Safety Guidelines & Load Management
*   **No Guaranteed Safety:** There is no single "safe" thickness. Safety depends on ice density relative to the load.
*   **Assess Load Weight:** Safety increases or decreases based on load weight and distribution.
*   **Watch for Dynamic Factors:** Ice conditions change constantly. Be aware of:
    *   Unintended stationary loads (objects left on ice).
    *   Vehicle speed.
    *   Other traffic or loads.
    *   Frequency of use (crossings, parking, etc.).
*   **Distribution:** Ensure weight is distributed properly to avoid breaking the ice.
