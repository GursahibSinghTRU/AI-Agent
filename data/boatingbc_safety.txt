# Safe Boating Guidelines (British Columbia)

## Key Risk Statistics
- **Drowning:** Over 80% of drowning victims in Canada could swim but were not wearing a flotation device.
- **Alcohol:** Alcohol is a factor in over 40% of boating fatalities.
- **Cold Water:** Significant risks exist early in the season or in exposed waters.

## Essential Safety Protocols
- **Personal Flotation Device (PFD):** Must be Canadian-approved and sized for each passenger. Must be **worn**, not just carried.
- **Impaired Operation:** Boating under the influence of alcohol or recreational drugs is illegal and prohibited.
- **Operator Card:** Anyone driving a motorized boat must hold a Pleasure Craft Operator Card.
- **Float Plan:** Share departure, destination, and return time with a responsible party before each trip.
- **Vessel Capability:** Ensure safety equipment, fuel, and experience levels match vessel capabilities and weather conditions.

## Shared Waterways Navigation
- **Vessel Types:** Freighters, BC Ferries, and Tug & Barges are difficult to maneuver and stop.
- **Safety Margin:** Maintain distance and adhere to rules of the road to accommodate larger, slow-reacting vessels.
- **Partnership:** Safety initiative involving Boating BC, Vancouver Fraser Port Authority, and BC Ferries.

## Resources
- **Reference:** Transport Canada’s Safe Boating Guide.
- **Training:** Online safe boating courses available for certification.
- **Equipment:** Minimum safety equipment lists available for specific activities (e.g., sailing, fishing, power boating).
