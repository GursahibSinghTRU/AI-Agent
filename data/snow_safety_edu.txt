# AdventureSmart Snow Safety & Education Program

## Overview
- **Objective:** Foster risk awareness for winter activities (skiing, snowboarding, snowshoeing, snowmobiling) to ensure safe experiences for children and groups.
- **Cost:** Free of charge to deliver.
- **Target Audience:** Children, students, and groups led by adults (parents, teachers, guides).
- **Provider:** BC AdventureSmart.

## Core Methodology: The 3 Ts
Presentations must cover these three key messaging pillars:
1.  **Trip Plan:** Route planning, terrain/condition/weather checks. Must leave a written plan (answering Who, What, When, Where, Why, How) with a contact person.
2.  **Training:** Learn the Alpine Responsibility Code. Complete activity-specific courses (ski/snowboard lessons).
3.  **Taking the Essentials:** Required equipment includes helmets (mandatory at most hills) and warm clothing (gloves, socks, hat).

## Curriculum Modules
- **Part 1: On-hill Safety:** Resort signs, symbols, and on-mountain protocols.
- **Part 2: Backcountry Safety:** Unmanaged space hazards, avalanche preparedness, additional training, and equipment.

## Delivery Guidelines
- **Presenter Options:** Delivered by trained volunteers or self-led by interested adults using the *Presenting Snow Safety & Education* guide.
- **Adaptability:** Content can be adapted for age groups and time allocation.
- **Sensitivity:** Presenters must discuss past winter recreation incidents with respect and use inclusive language.

## Resources & Contact
- **Helpline:** +1 866-972-7822
- **Media:** "A Little Respect—think first" video, "STOKE Video".
- **Registration:** Individuals must be associated with an organization and receive training to register as Outdoor Educators.
- **Request:** Submit presentation requests via the AdventureSmart website.
