# Canadian Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC) Compliance Summary

## Legal Requirements & Validity
- **Proof of Competency:** Mandatory for operating any engine-powered pleasure craft in Canada.
- **Valid Document:** Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC). Not technically a license but proof of competency.
- **Expiration:** Valid for life across all Canadian provinces and territories. No renewal fees or age limits.
- **Exemptions:** Pre-April 1999 course certificates, specific marine safety certificates, or a valid Boat Rental Safety Checklist (for rental periods).

## Certification Process
- **Accreditation:** Must be completed through a Transport Canada accredited provider.
- **Online Course Structure:**
  - 5 Chapters with 10-question quizzes each.
  - **Chapter Pass Mark:** 80%.
  - **Minimum Completion Time:** 3 hours.
- **Final Assessment:**
  - **Format:** 50 multiple-choice questions.
  - **Pass Mark:** 75%.
  - **Retries:** Unlimited attempts (24-hour cooldown); repeat course only required after two failures.
- **Delivery:** Immediate temporary digital certificate; permanent card mailed within weeks.

## Operational Safety Guidelines
- **Personal Protection:** Operators must wear proper life jackets and learn to swim.
- **Vessel Management:** Do not overload with passengers or equipment; follow proper anchoring techniques.
- **Pre-Departure:** Utilize Pleasure Craft Courtesy Check (PCCC) for safety verification.

## Administrative Information
- **Registration Validity:** 1 full year to complete course and test.
- **Lost Card:** Order replacements via accredited provider or Transport Canada database.
- **Registration Deadline:** Registration expires one year from payment date.
