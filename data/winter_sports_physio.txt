# Winter Sports Safety Guidelines

## Injury Prevention & Preparation
- **Warm-Up Routine:** Perform 10–15 minutes of dynamic stretches (e.g., lunges, leg swings) before activity to increase heart rate and flexibility. Avoid static stretching prior to physical exertion.
- **Equipment & Gear:** 
    - Wear appropriate footwear with secure fasteners.
    - Use helmets to prevent concussions and head injuries.
    - Snowboarders and skiers should wear wrist guards.
    - Ensure ski bindings are properly adjusted for fit and function.
    - Ice skaters must ensure boots and skates fit securely.
- **Activity Selection:** 
    - Choose sledding hills free from obstacles (trees, fences).
    - Select slopes matching current experience levels to avoid overexertion.
    - Use steering-designed sleds to reduce crash risks.
- **Physical Conditioning:** Strengthen core, knees, lower back, and legs. Focus on stability and balance exercises to reduce sprain and ligament tear risks.

## Injury Management
- **R.I.C.E. Protocol:** Apply Rest, Ice, Compression, and Elevation immediately for sprains and strains. Limit icing sessions to 20 minutes to prevent frostbite.
- **Recovery Strategy:** Immobilize and elevate affected joints (knees, ankles). Engage in gradual reintroduction to low-impact activities after muscle strains.
- **Professional Intervention:** Seek professional rehabilitation if rest does not resolve pain, or in cases of severe injuries (dislocations, fractures).

## Specific Sport Considerations
- **Skiing:** Focus on edge control and effective stopping. Knee injuries are common; prioritize stability and proper form.
- **Snowboarding:** Watch out for shoulder injuries and concussions. Maintain proper stance with knees slightly bent and distribute weight evenly.
- **Sledding:** Sit face-forward to reduce back injury risk. Keep feet forward to maintain steering control and avoid lying flat.
- **Skating:** Maintain upright stance with knees bent. Monitor surroundings to avoid accidental collisions and bumps.
