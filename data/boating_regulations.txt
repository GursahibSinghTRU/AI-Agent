# Boating Safety & Regulations Summary (Lifesaving Society Canada)

## Legal Requirements
- **Proof of Competency:** All Canadian operators of pleasure craft fitted with motors must carry a Pleasure Craft Operator (PCO) card.
- **Exceptions:** Proof of competency is not currently required in Nunavut or Northwest Territories.
- **Regulations:** Operators must abide by local bylaws and obey all signs and buoys (especially around dams and hydroelectric facilities).

## Personal Flotation Device (PFD) Statistics
- **Drowning Risk:** Approximately 80% of people who died in boating-related drownings were not wearing a lifejacket/PFD.
- **Fatality Attribution:** Not wearing a PFD accounts for 79% of boating deaths (for which PFD data was available).
- **Fishing Risk:** 34% of recreational boating fatalities involved fishing; PFDs are required even when trolling or stationary.
- **Vessel Risk:** Powerboats account for over 50% of recreational boating fatalities (canoeing 23%, kayaking 7%).

## Operational Safety Guidelines
- **Wear Requirement:** PFDs must be worn at all times; attempting to put one on after a capsize is often impossible.
- **Environmental Awareness:** Check forecasts and return to shore in bad weather or rough water.
- **Communication:** Carry a cell phone or VHF radio; inform others of destination and return time.
- **Boat Maintenance:** Check boat condition and ensure sufficient fuel before departure.
- **Behavior:** Do not speed; stay seated in small boats to prevent capsizing.
- **Equipment:** Do not rely solely on having a PFD on board; wearing is mandatory for survival.
