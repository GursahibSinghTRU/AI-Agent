# Mountain Biking Injury Prevention Guidelines

## Risk Profile
- **Injury Types:** Broken bones, organ damage, spinal injury.
- **Hazard Zones:** Gravel, rocks, logs, steep descents (e.g., Sea to Sky corridor).
- **Authority Warnings:** Vancouver Coastal Health reports an increase in mountain biking-related injuries.

## Protective Equipment Requirements
- **Essential:** Properly fitted helmet.
- **Standard PPE:** Elbow pads, knee pads, body armour.
- **Advanced PPE:** Neck brace (recommended for serious riders to prevent spinal injury).
- **Additional:** Protective backpack.

## Operational Safety Protocols
- **Risk Assessment:** Riders must know their ability level and limits.
- **Trail Selection:** Stay within safe zones appropriate for current skill set.
- **Behavioral Controls:** Do not attempt maneuvers due to peer pressure or social media influence.
- **Training:** Seek professional instruction before attempting jumps or increasing difficulty.
- **Mindset:** Focus on risk management and balance rather than avoidance.
