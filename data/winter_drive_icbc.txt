# Winter Driving Safety Guidelines (ICBC)

## Operational Safety Measures
- **Speed Control:** Reduce speed on slippery highways to mitigate ice and snow hazards.
- **Tire Maintenance:** Use winter tires; monitor tire pressure regularly as cold temperatures reduce it.
- **Vehicle Readiness:** Carry a winter emergency kit to handle situations where the vehicle is stranded or stuck.

## Essential Emergency Kit Inventory
- First aid kit
- Emergency food and water supplies
- Spare warm clothing
- Light sources: Flares, matches, lighter, or candles
- Recovery tools: Shovel, traction mat, sand, or non-clumping kitty litter
- Electrical aid: Battery jumper cables

## Additional Safety Resources
- **Highway Regulations:** Information on routes requiring winter tires or chains (DriveBC).
- **Educational Programs:** Winter Driving Safety Alliance (Shift Into Winter).
- **Specific Safety Contexts:** Child seat safety in winter, driving in poor conditions, and winter tire tips.

## Service Support Locations
- Driver licensing offices
- Autoplan brokers and claim centres
- Health care providers
- Repair facilities: Collision, glass, commercial, and service centres
- Emergency service locators
