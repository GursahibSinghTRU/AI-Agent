# Mountain Biking Risk Summary (Cycling BC)

## Terrain & Environment
- **Off-Road Operations:** Rough terrain including forests, mountains, deserts, fire roads, and singletrack.
- **Isolation Risk:** Riders frequently operate miles from civilization; immediate help may not be available.
- **Equipment Characteristics:** Heavier bicycle weight-to-rider ratio due to durability requirements (wide knobby tires, large tubing, suspension systems).

## Operational Hazards
- **Self-Reliance:** Riders must possess self-reliance skills to repair bikes or fix flats to avoid being stranded.
- **Group Dynamics:** Club rides and group rides are common, requiring coordination for multiple users.
- **Competence Verification:** UCI International License categories define ability levels (Novice, Intermediate, Expert, Pro-Elite).

## Safety Controls & Mitigations
- **Protective Gear:** UCI regulations mandate protective clothing standards for racing.
- **Trail Design:** Advocacy for "Whistler Standards" (Environmental and Technical Trail Features) to ensure safe planning.
- **Eligibility:** Riders must hold a UCI license from the last season for upgrades; cannot downgrade mid-season.

## Incident Management & Resources
- **Reporting:** Accident Report Form available for incident documentation.
- **Insurance:** Insurance Info & FAQs accessible for rider coverage verification.
- **Compliance:** Safe Sport, Ride Leader Training, and Club Resources available for liability and safety management.
