# Campaign: Know Before You SNOW
**Organizations:** BC Search and Rescue Association (BCSARA), BC AdventureSmart, North Shore Rescue, Partners (Ski Areas, Land Managers, Schools)
**Region:** British Columbia (North Shore)
**Duration:** November through March (Winter Season)
**Objective:** Reduce SAR impacts, promote informed winter travel, and strengthen safety culture.

## Risk Statistics (2023–2025)
- **Winter Hiking:** Accounts for approximately 74% of all winter incidents.
- **Winter Sports:** Combined skiing, snowboarding, and snowshoeing incidents rose to 13.5%.
- **Terrain Risk:** Leaving ski resort boundaries automatically enters avalanche terrain.

## Safety Protocols
- **The 3Ts:** Plan (Trip Plan), Train (Skills), Take the Essentials (Gear).
- **Alpine Responsibility Code (ARC):** Mandatory compliance; do not bypass lift lines or resort boundaries.
- **Backcountry Requirements:** Travel via designated entry points requires self-sufficiency (24-hour survival gear, satellite device, training).
- **Visibility:** Wear bright clothing for SAR detection.
- **Decision Making:** Turn around early if weather or conditions deteriorate.
- **Solo Travel:** Never travel alone in hazardous terrain.

## Emergency Procedures
- **Activation:** Call 911 or Emergency Coordination Centre (1-800-663-3456) for satellite devices.
- **Reporting:** Do **not** wait 24 hours to report overdue or missing persons.
- **Cost:** Search and Rescue services in BC are provided free of charge.
- **Activation Authority:** SAR teams are activated by requesting agencies (Police, EHS) only.
