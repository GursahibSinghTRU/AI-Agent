# Winter Pet Safety & Hazard Mitigation
**Source:** Lakeview Animal Hospital / LifeLearn Animal Health
**Category:** Environmental Hazards, Chemical Safety, Emergency Preparedness

## Thermal & Exposure Risks
*   **Hypothermia & Frostbite:** All pets face risk regardless of fur type. Limit outdoor exposure time in snow/cold.
*   **Shelter Requirements:** Outdoor animals need a dry, draft-free, insulated shelter raised off the ground with a watertight entrance.
*   **Activity Limits:** Shorten walks for older, young, short-haired, or arthritic pets. Monitor for shivering or weakness.
*   **Nutrition:** Maintain energy levels for heat retention without overfeeding during periods of reduced activity.

## Chemical & Physical Hazards
*   **Road Salts:** Ice melters/chemicals can burn paw pads. Wipe paws and underbellies after outdoor activity; use booties.
*   **Antifreeze:** Highly toxic with a sweet taste. Store out of reach and clean spills immediately.
*   **Vehicle Safety:** Never leave pets unattended in unheated cars (rapid cooling). Warn wildlife from engines before starting.
*   **Ice Safety:** Avoid unknown frozen bodies of water; verify ice thickness for safe traversal.

## Emergency & Behavioral Monitoring
*   **Stockpiling:** Maintain supplies (food, water, medications) for at least one week during storm events.
*   **Behavioral Watch:** Observe for changes in personality, habits, or health indicators.
*   **Medical Disclaimer:** This information supports general safety awareness and does not replace veterinary consultation.
