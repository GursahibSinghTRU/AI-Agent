# TRU Onboarding & Key Policies Guide

Welcome to Thompson Rivers University! Whether you're joining us as a faculty member or staff, your contributions are essential to our vibrant and inclusive community.

## Welcome Message

At TRU, we believe that a great start leads to a great journey. People and Culture is here to support you every step of the way. From your first day to your first semester and beyond, we're committed to helping you feel informed, connected, and empowered.

We've created a comprehensive onboarding experience to guide you through key steps, resources, and opportunities.

This is a flexible resource—no quizzes, no deadlines. Explore at your own pace and revisit anytime.

## New Employee Onboarding Checklist - For Managers

### Before Start Date

- Ensure the new employee does not start employment without a signed contract returned to People & Culture
- Ensure the new employee has completed all hiring paperwork and (if applicable) benefits information with People & Culture
- Call the new employee to confirm the finalization of the process, officially welcoming them to TRU and the department
- Provide them with the name, phone number and email of any other required points of contact
- Discuss what they can expect from their first few weeks at TRU:
  - Time they should arrive
  - Location of the office
  - Where to park
  - What to bring
  - Office attire
- Create a department orientation schedule to arrange introductions and tours
- Send an announcement to your team and colleagues introducing the new employee and advising their start date
- Discuss training roles and responsibilities with required team members
- Prepare the new employee's workspace:
  - Ensure space is clean and cleared
  - Remove past incumbent's belongings
  - Make it a welcoming space equipped with required materials
- Make arrangements for the new employee's name on the office door
- Submit an ASAR via the IT Services Portal
- Email the IT Service Desk to request telephone/voicemail setup
- Order business cards by emailing Print Services (if needed)
- Confirm inclusion to relevant email distribution lists
- Confirm access to departmental Outlook mailboxes and appropriate electronic files
- Prepare the TRU Key Requisition Form (if employee requires a key)

### First Day & Week

- Welcome your new team member to TRU and the department
- Take time to relate and show empathy towards starting something new; share your own TRU journey
- Show them their workstation/office
- Introduce them to team/department members
- Briefly describe department structure, organization and priorities and remind the employee how they will contribute to departmental outcomes
- Confirm parking is sorted and offer assistance or refer to TRU's Transportation resources
- Discuss building/office access and alarm system
- Tour meeting rooms, washrooms, lunchrooms, designated smoking areas, etc.
- Take the new employee on a tour of campus
- Introduce them to colleagues and other departments they may need to interact with
- Provide login instructions and where to access electronic files
- Review OneTRU and myTRU including timesheet reporting
- Review standard voicemail and email greetings/signatures

## Completing Your Journey

We know starting a new role comes with a lot to take in, and we hope this guide has helped you feel more confident, connected, and supported. Remember, onboarding is a journey, not a sprint. Take your time, ask questions, and lean on the People and Culture team whenever you need.

If you ever feel stuck or curious, reach out to onboarding@tru.ca—we're just an email away.
