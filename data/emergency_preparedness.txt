# Wilderness Safety & Emergency Preparedness Guidelines
**Source:** British Columbia Ministry of Transportation and Infrastructure / Emergency Management BC (EMBC)
**Document Type:** Information Bulletin
**Publication Date:** December 17, 2016 (Historical Guidelines)
**Relevance:** General backcountry safety for fieldwork/recreation in BC wilderness.

## Core Safety Protocols
*   **Never Hike Alone:** Travel in groups.
*   **Trip Planning:** Leave a trip plan (destination, route, expected return time) with friends or family. Adhere strictly to the plan.
*   **Stay On Trail:** Do not venture out of bounds or off marked trails.
*   **Monitor Time:** Track distance traveled and sunset times. Turn back before darkness; carry a flashlight (darkness comes early in winter).
*   **If Lost:**
    *   Do not keep moving.
    *   Stay put and wait for help.
    *   Do not move downhill (dangerous terrain risk).
*   **Communication:** Carry a signaling device (whistle), fully charged cellphone, personal locator beacon (PLB), compass, or GPS unit.

## Equipment Essentials
*   **Water:** Carry extra water.
*   **Clothing:** Loose-fitting, layered, lightweight clothing.
    *   *Materials:* Wool and silk preferred.
    *   *Outer Layer:* Tightly woven, water-repellent material for wind protection.
*   **Survival Gear:** Shelter and fire-starting materials for overnight warmth.

## Hypothermia Prevention (COLD Acronym)
| Letter | Action | Detail |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **C** | **Cover** | Wear scarf, hat, mittens, gloves, balaclava. |
| **O** | **Overexertion** | Avoid excessive sweating; wet clothing + cold = rapid heat loss. |
| **L** | **Layers** | Wear loose-fitting, layered clothing. |
| **D** | **Dry** | Get out of wet clothing immediately. Keep hands/feet dry. |

## Hypothermia Warning Signs
*   Constant shivering
*   Confusion
*   Poor decision-making (e.g., removing warm clothes)
*   Drowsiness
*   Shallow breathing
