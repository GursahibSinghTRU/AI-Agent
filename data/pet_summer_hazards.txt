## Source: BC SPCA (May 9, 2024)
## Topic: Summer Hazards for Pets

### Heat & Hydration Risks
*   **Vehicular Safety:** Never leave pets unattended in hot vehicles, even briefly.
*   **Pavement Burns:** Schedule walks during cooler times; hot surfaces can burn paw pads.
*   **Heatstroke Indicators:** Excessive panting, drooling, agitation, increased heart rate. Immediate cooling and veterinary consultation required.
*   **Hydration:** Provide constant access to cool, clean water and shade.

### Chemical & Dietary Toxins
*   **Food Hazards:** High-risk items include cooked bones (choking/splintering), onions, garlic, grapes, chocolate, and corn on cob.
*   **Gardening Hazards:** Secure fertilizers, lawn products, and toxic plant bulbs from pet access.
*   **Sunscreen:** Consult a vet before application; prevent ingestion due to harmful ingredients. Use pet-specific or baby formula.

### Biological Hazards
*   **Ticks:** Perform thorough checks (ears, armpits, between toes) after outdoor exposure. Symptoms may take 7–21 days to appear (e.g., Lyme disease). Use veterinarian-approved preventatives.
*   **Stings:** Remove stingers by scraping (do not squeeze). Apply cold compress. Seek immediate aid for anaphylactic signs (vomiting, pale gums).

### Outdoor Activity Precautions
*   **Vaccinations:** Maintain current status against wildlife-borne viruses (rabies, leptospirosis).
*   **Water Safety:** Equip dogs with properly fitted life vests for boating or swimming.
*   **Identification:** Register pets with Pet Registry for easier identification if lost.
*   **Hiking Supplies:** Pack collapsible bowls, extra water, and pet-friendly sunscreen for long hikes.
