# Swimming Safety Knowledge Summary

**Injuries, drowning, and deaths are preventable** by following these safety rules. These guidelines apply to all water bodies, from beaches and lakes to private pools and bathtubs.

### 🛑 General Conduct
*   **Follow Signs:** Always read and obey posted safety information to protect yourself and maintain pool hygiene.
*   **Buddy System:** Never swim alone. Have a strong swimmer with you, even if a lifeguard is present.
*   **Walking:** Walk on pool decks; never run. Surfaces are extremely slippery when wet.
*   **Entrance/Exit:** Use handrails. Avoid climbing on slippery or unstable surfaces.

### 🌊 Water Safety & Ability
*   **Know Your Limits:** Swim only in areas where you feel comfortable. Do not go deeper or farther than you can handle.
*   **At Beaches:** Stay inside marked swimming areas or close to the shore.
*   **Look Before You Leap:** Never dive or jump into shallow water. Always check the area for underwater obstacles, logs, or "No Diving" signs.
*   **Head Injuries:** Play carefully. Losing consciousness after hitting your head against walls or rocks can lead to drowning.

### ⚠️ Specific Hazards
*   **Suction Points (Drains/Skimers):** Never play around pool drains or skimmers. Fingers, toes, hair, or limbs can be caught in the suction.
    *   *Prevention:* Tie back long hair or wear a swim cap.
*   **Hot Tubs/Whirlpools:** Never submerge your head underwater in a whirlpool or hot tub.
*   **Underwater Traps:** Equipment like railings, ladders, or filters can create trapping spaces. Alert a supervisor if you notice dangerous equipment.
*   **Natural Hazards:** Lakes, rivers, and oceans may have unknown dangers (sudden drop-offs, algae blooms, tidal currents). Obey warnings and ask locals about trouble spots.

### 🚫 Substance Use
*   **No Alcohol:** Do not drink alcohol around water bodies, hot tubs, or beaches.
    *   *Risk:* Alcohol slows reaction time and can cause you to fall asleep, leading to drowning or worse accidents.

### 🎓 Emergency Preparedness
*   **Take First Aid:** Training helps you respond to emergencies. Focus on learning:
    *   Rescue breathing
    *   CPR (adult and child)
*   **Water Quality:** For natural bodies of water, contact local government or health units for recreational water quality testing info.

> **Note:** All public pools must comply with the **BC Pool Regulation**.
