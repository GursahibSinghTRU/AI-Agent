# Hiking Safety & Preparation Guidelines
**Source:** Parks Canada (2024)
**Scope:** Outdoor risk management for hiking activities.

## Core Responsibilities
*   **Self-Sufficiency:** Individuals are responsible for their own safety and must remain self-sufficient during all conditions and emergencies.
*   **Emergency Protocols:** Review visitor safety and emergency procedures for the specific location prior to departure.

## Pre-Hike Planning
*   **Trail Selection:** Match trail difficulty (Green Circle/Easy, Blue Square/Moderate, Black Diamond/Difficult, Double Diamond/Route) to group experience and physical ability.
*   **Pacing:** Estimate travel time conservatively. Average beginner speed is 2–2.5 km/h.
*   **Navigation:** Carry physical maps, compass, and GPS tools. Do not rely solely on smartphone GPS due to limited cell service.
*   **Trip Plan:** Inform a contact of location, expected return time, and emergency contacts before departure.

## Safety Equipment & Gear
*   **Essentials:** First aid kit, whistle, flashlight, fire starter, multi-tool.
*   **Footwear:** Waterproof, supportive shoes with grip for roots/rocks. Avoid flip-flops.
*   **Clothing:** Lightweight, breathable layers for changing weather. Include sun protection (sunglasses, sunscreen, hat).

## During the Hike
*   **Hydration:** Consume ~0.5 L water per hour. Do not wait until thirsty.
*   **Nutrition:** Bring high-energy snacks; pack complete meals for hikes exceeding a few hours.
*   **Wildlife:** Maintain distance (100m for bears/cougars, 30m for moose/elk/deer). Do not feed or approach wildlife.
*   **Environment:** Stay on marked trails to protect vegetation and wildlife. Dispose of all trash and recyclables.
*   **Group Safety:** Avoid solo hiking unless experienced or on easy, popular trails.

## Post-Hike
*   **Leave No Trace:** Remove all waste from the site or use designated bins.
