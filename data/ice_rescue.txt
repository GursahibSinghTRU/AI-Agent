# Ice Safety and Self-Rescue Guidelines

## Preparation & Equipment
- **Clothing:** Wear waterproof, buoyant, and insulating clothing (floater suit, survival suit, or drysuit). Carry spare dry socks/clothes in a waterproof bag to prevent wet clothing complications.
- **Safety Gear:**
    - **Cell Phone:** Essential for emergencies; keep in a waterproof sleeve/ziplock.
    - **Whistle:** Lightweight; use two short blasts for attention, three long blasts for emergency.
    - **Ice Picks:** Compact leverage tools for self-extraction.
    - **Leverage:** Walking sticks or ski poles can assist in pulling weight.
- **Planning:** Prepare for longer exposure than planned; ensure rescue gear is accessible.

## Physiological Response (1-10-1 Rule)
- **1 Minute – Cold Shock:** Gasping and hyperventilation are normal. Priority is controlling breathing and keeping head above water.
- **10 Minutes – Cold Incapacitation:** Limited meaningful movement remains. Attempt self-rescue immediately. If unsuccessful, pull up onto ice ASAP and remain still to conserve energy.
- **1 Hour – Hypothermia:** Unconsciousness likely. Survival depends on body fat, insulation, and maintaining airway above water (e.g., hair freezing to ice).

## Self-Rescue Procedure
1. **Horizontal Position:** Do not pull up vertically. Kick legs to get horizontal along the water surface.
2. **Exit:** Pull forward onto the ice from the path used to fall through (if stable).
3. **Weight Distribution:**
    - Lay on stomach immediately to reduce pressure on the hole.
    - Roll away from the hole initially.
    - Transition to crawling only once confident in ice integrity.
    - Walk only when ice is confirmed stable.
4. **Thermal Management:** Remove wet clothes immediately. Replace with dry layers. Consume calories and seek warmth.

## Training & Disclaimer
- **Limitation:** This guide is not a substitute for hands-on practice or certification.
- **Recommendation:** Complete Ice Safety & Rescue certification for practical skills, equipment assessment, and real-world ice evaluation.
- **Prevention:** Always assess ice conditions before travel.
