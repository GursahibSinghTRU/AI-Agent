# Cold Water and Ice Safety Summary
**Source:** The Lifesaving Society (Ontario Branch)
**Topic:** Ice Safety, Cold Water Survival, and Drowning Prevention

## Key Statistics
- **Seasonality:** Approximately 35% of drownings in Canada occur from October to April.
- **Temperature Risk:** Even in summer, Canadian lakes/rivers are usually below 20°C. Hypothermia can set in within 1 hour.
- **Demographics:** Males account for 80% of unintentional ice-related fatalities; almost all fatalities occur among adults.
- **Activity Risk:** Nearly half of fatalities occur during recreational activity. ~40% occur while snowmobiling.
- **Location:** ~50% on lakes/ponds; ~33% on flowing water.
- **Alcohol:** Contributing factor in 1 in 3 fatalities.
- **Socio-Factor:** 50% of fatalities occur when the individual is alone.

## Minimum Ice Thickness Requirements
| Activity | Minimum Thickness |
| :--- | :--- |
| Single Person | 4 inches (10 cm) |
| Snowmobile / ATV | 5 inches (12 cm) |
| Single Car | 8-12 inches (20-30 cm) |
| Full-Size Pickup | 12-15 inches (30-38 cm) |

## Safety Protocols
- **Equipment:** Wear a thermal protection buoyant suit or lifejacket/PFD. Carry a safety kit (lighter, ice picks, rope, phone, whistle).
- **Planning:** Never go on the ice alone. Inform someone of destination and return time. Avoid travel at night or low visibility.
- **Avoid:** Unfamiliar paths, slushy/thawed/refrozen ice, layered/rotten ice, and ice near moving water.
- **Behavior:** Stay sober. Do not attempt to rescue dogs falling into cold water.
- **Myths Debunked:**
    - Snow insulates and weakens ice.
    - Thick ice can be fragile ("rotten").
    - Clothing traps air and aids buoyancy initially.
    - Swimming ability is secondary in cold water survival.

## Emergency Procedure: If Ice Breaks
**1-10-1 Rule:**
1. **Minute:** Control breathing.
2. **10 Minutes:** Attempt to get out of the water before losing mobility.
3. **1 Hour:** Survival window before hypothermia (with PFD).

**Actions:**
- Do not panic.
- Turn toward the unbroken ice immediately.
- Disperse weight; do not stand up.
- Swim/crawl on your stomach to shore.
- Roll, crawl, or shimmy away from the break.

## Policy & Training
- **Swim to Survive:** Defines minimum skills needed to survive an unexpected deep water fall.
- **Lifejackets:** Mandatory wearing of functional lifejackets/PFDs is advocated for watercraft less than 5.5m without a cabin.
