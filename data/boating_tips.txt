# Boating Safety & Risk Guidelines (Canada)

## Legal Mandates
- **Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC):** Mandatory for operating any motorized pleasure craft. Must be carried on board. Lifetime validity.
- **Boating Under the Influence (BUI):** Classified as a Criminal Code offense. Alcohol consumption on board requires anchoring and permanent sleeping/kitchen facilities in most provinces.
- **Personal Flotation Devices (PFDs):** Required for all onboard personnel. Majority of drowning victims were not wearing PFDs.

## Operational Protocols
- **Pre-Departure:** Complete maintenance and safety checklists; verify weather conditions (TV/radio forecasts).
- **Float Plan:** Inform a shore contact of the trip itinerary, trip leader details, passenger list, and communication equipment onboard.
- **Crew Readiness:** Designate a valid PCOC-holding Assistant Skipper to ensure navigation capability if primary operator is incapacitated.
- **Skill Requirements:** Operators and crew should possess swimming ability. Encourage additional safety courses beyond mandatory PCOC.

## Safety Resources
- **Vessel Safety Check:** Free courtesy examinations available via Canadian Power and Sail Squadron (CPS-ECP) in partnership with the Office of Boating Safety.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Verify provincial carriage restrictions (e.g., OPP, SQ, RCMP).

## Immediate Risk Mitigation
- **Speed Management:** Operate at safe speeds at all times, especially in crowded areas.
- **Vessel Awareness:** Maintain distance from large vessels with restricted maneuverability.
- **Navigational Aids:** Respect buoys and aids placed to ensure safety.
- **Emergency Prep:** Ensure all safety equipment is functional and accessible before departure.
