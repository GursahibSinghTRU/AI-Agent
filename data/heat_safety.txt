# Heat Safety Guidelines (Island Health Reference)

## 🚨 Severe Symptoms Requiring 9-1-1
- **Temperature:** Core body temp >39°C (102°F)
- **Physical Signs:** Hot/pale skin, light-headedness, fainting, confusion, disorientation, seizures, unconsciousness, not sweating.
- **Vital Signs:** Rapid breathing, rapid heart rate.
- **Immediate Action:** Call 9-1-1, move to cool place, cool with water and fanning.

## ⚠️ Mild to Moderate Symptoms
- **Signs:** Heavy sweating, severe headache, muscle cramps, extreme thirst, dark urine.
- **Action:** Move to cooler environment, hydrate, wear wet shirt or apply damp towels.

## 🌡 Indoor Temperature Thresholds
| Indoor Temp | Risk Level | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **≤ 26°C** | Generally Safe | Standard precautions. |
| **26°C – 31°C** | Moderate Risk | Monitor susceptible populations. |
| **> 31°C** | High Risk | Leave for cooler location if possible. |

## 👥 At-Risk Populations
- Age 60 or older
- Living alone
- Pre-existing conditions (Diabetes, Heart, Respiratory, Mental Health, Substance Use)
- Pregnant individuals
- Infants and young children
- Limited mobility or marginally housed individuals
- Outdoor/Hot environment workers

## 🔒 Mitigation Strategies
- **Hydration:** Drink fluids proactively; avoid alcohol, caffeine, and excess sugar.
- **Indoor Management:** 
  - Close windows/doors during peak heat; open only when outdoors is cooler.
  - Sleep in coolest room/basement; use AC for sleeping areas.
  - Fans help move air but do not lower core body temperature alone.
- **Vehicle Safety:** NEVER leave children or pets in parked cars.
- **Social Support:** Establish "hot weather buddy" check-ins (more than once per day during events).

## 🌫 Smoke and Heat Co-occurrence
- **Priority:** Prioritize cooling over smoke inhalation risks (overheating is a greater immediate threat).
- **Indoor Air:** Keep windows closed if possible; use AC for filtered air.
- **Locations:** Seek cooling centers, malls, or libraries if home is too hot.

## 📞 Resources & Contacts
- **Emergency Info BC:** EmergencyInfoBC
- **HealthLine BC:** 8-1-1
- **EmergencyMapBC:** For cooling/misting centers
- **External Guides:** Gov of Canada Extreme Heat Events, HealthLink BC "Beat the Heat"
