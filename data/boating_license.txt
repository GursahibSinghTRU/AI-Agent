# British Columbia Boating Safety & Legal Requirements

## Certification Requirement
- **Mandatory Card:** A Pleasure Craft Operator Card (PCOC) is legally required to operate any motorized vessel on Canadian/BC waters.
- **Driver's Licence:** A standard BC driver's licence is **not** a valid substitute for a boating licence.
- **Validity:** The PCOC is valid for life and does not expire.

## Age & Engine Power Restrictions
- **Under 12 Years:** Maximum 10 horsepower (unsupervised operation allowed).
- **Ages 12–16:** Maximum 40 horsepower (unsupervised operation allowed).
- **Under 16 Years:** Personal Watercraft (PWC) operation is strictly prohibited.
- **Minimum Age:** No minimum age requirement to obtain the PCOC.

## Prohibited Conduct
- **Alcohol:** Operating a vessel while under the influence of alcohol is illegal and mirrors standard drinking-and-driving laws.
- **Proof of Competency:** Must be carried on board at all times while operating a motorized vessel.

## Penalties & Enforcement
- **Fines:** Operators caught without proof of competency may face fines of $250 or more.

## Exemptions from PCOC Requirement
- Completed boating safety course prior to April 1, 1999.
- Rentals equipped with a completed and signed safety checklist (valid only for rental duration).
- Holders of recognized marine certificates or equivalencies approved by Transport Canada.
- Non-residents operating unregistered pleasure craft in BC waters for less than 45 consecutive days.
- Members of First Nations engaged in daily living or subsistence activities (e.g., hunting/fishing).

## Additional Licensing Notes
- **Fishing:** A separate recreational fishing licence is required based on freshwater or saltwater location.
- **Commercial:** PCOC does not suffice for commercial chartering or tour operations; additional certifications are necessary.
