# Winter Sport Safety Guidelines (Source: ACEP)

## General Outdoor Safety
*   **Clothing:** Dress in layers to adjust to temperature changes. Protect frostbite-prone areas (fingers, nose).
*   **Hydration:** Maintain fluid intake even in cold weather.
*   **Sun Protection:** Apply sunscreen; snow reflects UV rays, increasing burn risk.
*   **Supervision:** Adults must supervise younger children.

## Sledding
*   **Equipment Inspection:** Check sleds for sharp edges, jagged surfaces, or protruding rivets.
*   **Terrain Check:** Inspect the sliding area before descent; teach children how to stop or bail off the sled in an emergency.
*   **Prohibited Action:** Never hook sleds onto motorized vehicles.

## Snowmobiling
*   **Operation:** Know how to operate the specific model before driving; always ensure a full tank of gas.
*   **PPE:** Wear goggles, helmets with chin straps, and protective clothing.
*   **Pre-Ride:** Conduct a safety check before each ride.
*   **Terrain:** Identify hidden hazards (fences, gullies, rocks, open water, thin ice).
*   **Protocol:** Never ride alone or attempt stunts. Inform others of intended plans (location and return time). Remain alert to noise masking other traffic.

## Skiing & Snowboarding
*   **Head Protection:** Wear a helmet; head injuries are the leading cause of death and serious injury.
*   **Wrist Protection:** Wear wrist guards; falls are the most common cause of wrist fractures.
*   **Footwear:** Use hard-shell boots. Snowboarders are at higher risk for ankle injuries with soft-shell boots.
*   **Training:** Take lessons from a qualified instructor. Avoid stunts without proper instruction, practice, and protection.

## Skating
*   **Gear:** Wear skates that fit comfortably and provide adequate ankle support. Sharpen blades professionally at the start of each season.
*   **Environment:** Skate only on specially prepared areas where ice strength is verified. Never skate on frozen ponds without safety signage. Check for cracks, holes, and debris.
*   **Skills:** Learn basic skills including how to stop and fall safely.
*   **Supervision:** Do not skate alone. Younger and less experienced skaters require appropriate safety gear and adult supervision.
