# Water Safety Guidelines (Kamloops Region)

## Swimming Locations & Supervision
- **Supervised Sites:** The safest option is a supervised pool. For natural water, Riverside Park is the only designated swimming area.
- **Lifeguard Duty:** Active July 1 to mid-August, 1:00–5:00 PM, 7 days/week (weather permitting).
- **Risk Warning:** Swimming in rivers (South/North Thompson) outside designated areas or outside duty hours is at your own risk.

## Environmental Hazards
- **Cold Water Shock:** Sudden immersion in cold water affects breathing and heart rate, increasing drowning risk even on warm days.
- **River Hazards:** Fast-moving currents, drop-offs, waves, rapids, underwater obstacles, and debris.
- **Environmental Checks:** Monitor weather changes; temperature and wind impact swimming difficulty.
- **Visibility:** Avoid swimming in low light or at night to ensure swimmer visibility.

## Personal Safety & Equipment
- **Life Jackets:** Mandatory for non-swimmers, children, and pets.
- **Buddy System:** Swim with a buddy whenever possible.
- **Floating:** Do not tie floats together; risk of entanglement around bridge abutments or docks.
- **Protection:** Wear sunscreen and protective clothing to prevent heat illness.
- **Hydration:** Bring ample water; fatigue + heat + water activities increases danger.

## Alcohol & Boating Safety
- **Alcohol:** Avoid alcohol (involved in 38% of BC boating deaths and 40% of Canadian drowning fatalities).
- **Licenses:** Only licensed individuals may operate a boat.
- **Planning:** Tell someone your destination and return time. Carry a safety kit.

## Emergency & Education
- **Distress:** Call 911 for water or marine emergencies.
- **Courses:** Take lifesaving courses (e.g., Bronze Medallion, Bronze Cross, National Lifeguard).
- **Supervision:** Keep children within arms' reach.

## Resources
