# Cold Weather Safety & Risk Mitigation Guidelines

## Risk Monitoring & Alerts
- **Alert Sources:** Environment and Climate Change Canada (ECCC) and WeatherCAN app.
- **Alert Types:** Arctic outflow warnings (Coastal B.C.), Extreme cold warnings, Snow/freezing rain alerts.
- **Risk Reality:** Individuals are at risk even without official alerts, particularly vulnerable populations.

## High-Risk Vulnerable Populations
- People experiencing homelessness.
- Individuals with poverty-related heating challenges (inadequate insulation/heat sources).
- People using substances (especially opioids).
- Outdoor workers or participants in winter activities.
- Individuals with limited mobility, disabilities, or medical conditions restricting blood flow.
- Older adults, infants, and young children.

## Safety Precautions & Prevention
- **Carbon Monoxide Warning:** Never use gas-burning appliances (barbeques, outdoor patio heaters, generators) indoors.
- **Disruption Planning:** Prepare for transportation delays and power outages.
- **Personal Protection:** Wear loose-fitting, dry layers. Keep head and neck covered.

## Hypothermia Response
- **Definition:** Core body temperature below 37°C; organs cannot function properly.
- **Key Symptoms:**
  - Cold skin, fumbling hands.
  - Shivering (may be absent in infants).
  - Exhaustion, slurred speech, confusion, memory loss.
  - Slow/shallow breathing, clumsiness, drowsiness, weak pulse, unconsciousness.
- **Action Plan:**
  - **Severe/Unconscious:** Call **911** immediately. Assume hypothermia until checked.
  - **Alert/Conscious:** Call **811** for guidance or seek medical care.
  - **Warming Protocol:** Move to warmer location, remove wet clothing, warm center of body first (chest, neck, head, groin). Use electric blankets, warm water bottles, or reflective blankets.
  - **Fluids:** Warm beverages only for conscious individuals; avoid alcohol. Do not give liquids to unconscious persons.
  - **Note:** Some overdose cases present with hypothermia symptoms; assume both conditions may exist.

## Frostbite Response
- **Symptoms:** White/grayish-yellow skin, waxy/firm texture, numbness.
- **Immediate Action:** Move indoors or protect exposed skin immediately.
- **Medical Care:** Consult a health care provider.
- **Warming Warning:** Only warm the affected area if the person can stay warm for a significant period to avoid further tissue damage.

## External Resources
- HealthLinkBC: Carbon Monoxide Poisoning, Cold Temperature Exposure.
- Canadian Red Cross: Cold-Related Emergencies.
- Prepared B.C.: Severe Winter Weather Preparedness.
