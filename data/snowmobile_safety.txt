# Snowmobiling Risk & Safety Guidelines

## Incident Statistics
- **Annual Impact:** Over 14,000 injuries and 200 deaths reported globally per year.
- **Common Injuries:** Fractures, dislocations, and concussions.

## Operational Safety Best Practices
- **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE):** Mandatory use of helmets, goggles, gloves, and appropriate winter clothing.
- **Terrain Management:** Avoid unfamiliar terrain and frozen water bodies (lakes/rivers) due to structural failure risks.
- **Regulatory Compliance:** Adhere strictly to posted trail signs, speed limits, and local access laws.
- **Riding Techniques:**
  - Slow down during turns; avoid sudden braking on icy surfaces.
  - Maintain safe speeds to allow adequate reaction time for obstacles.
  - Verify jump safety before attempting; land with knees bent to absorb impact.

## Training and Preparedness
- **Courses:** Enroll in snowmobile safety courses to learn maintenance, emergency preparedness, and terrain navigation.
- **Emergency Protocol:**
  - Remain calm to prevent rash decisions.
  - Call emergency services (911/local) or signal for help using a whistle/shouting if mobile signal is unavailable.
  - Stay in place unless the path to safety is verified and secure.
